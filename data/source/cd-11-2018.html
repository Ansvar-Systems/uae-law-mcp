التقديم
هيئة الامارات للمواصفات والمقاييس هي هيئة وطنية ومن مهامها الرئيسية إعداد وإصدار المواصفات القياسية الاماراتية واللوائح حيث تتولى إعدادها لجان فنية متخصصة تتشكل من الجهات المختصة الحكومية والخاصة.
قامت الهيئة من خلال فريق عمل فني شكلته لهذا الغرض بالتعاون مع شركة أدنوك للتوزيع بإعداد مشروع تحديث هذه اللائحة (الإصدار الثاني) الخاصة بإجراءات تحويل المركبات للعمل بالغاز الطبيعي في الدولة وكذلك المركبات التي تعمل بالغاز الطبيعي المضغوط والمُوَرّدة من المصنعيين مباشرة. وتضمن هذا الاصدار مراجعة شاملة لهذه اللائحة كما تم إضافة الدليل رقم ٧ وملاحقه والخاص بإعادة فحص اسطوانات الغاز الطبيعي المضغوط المستخدمة في كامل النظام، المثبة في المركبات ومحطات التعبئة الثابتة او المتنقلة.
وتم تعميم مشروع اللائحة المحدثة على الجهات المسؤولة عن تنفيذ مشروع تحويل المركبات للغاز الطبيعي في الدولة وتمت مناقشة جميع الملاحظات الواردة وأخذها في الاعتبار.
وتم اعتماد المشروع النهائي لهذه اللائحة من مجلس إدارة الهيئة وصدرت بموجب قرار رئيس مجلس الادارة رقم (11) لسنة 2018 وستلغي هذه اللائحة وتحل محل الاصدار الاول لـ "اللائحة الاماراتية لإجراءات تحويل المركبات للغاز الطبيعي "والصادر بموجب قرار رئيس مجلس الادارة رقم 53 لسنة 2014.

3- Purpose
The purpose of this Regulation is to establish and operate a scheme regulating vehicle conversion into natural gas as well as OEM NGVs in the UAE.
4- Scope
- The scope of this regulation comprises the following areas:
- Legal criteria for the use of natural gas components and their installation into vehicles
- Stakeholders and their roles
- Certification of NGV RVW
- Approval of converted vehicles and OEM vehicles using Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) in their propulsion system
- Training and certification of the NGV specialists and the responsible persons
- Inspections and certification of NGVs
- RVW requirements (safety, operation and certification)
- Labeling and marking
- Cylinder Re-inspection Requirements

UAE Requirements of Natural Gas Vehicles - Guidelines and Procedures
I- Pressurized gas systems and pressure vessels
1- Motor vehicles which are fitted with special equipment or components for use with compressed natural gas (CNG) in their drive system, must as a result of converting be approved in accordance with the provisions of the regulations stated in the Appendix 6 of this regulation. Before the first vehicle registration the respective type approval documentation must be submitted to a final Executer/Executer nominated TPI to accept the vehicle’s UNECE Type Approval resulting in a vehicle certification.
2- Special retrofit systems for using compressed natural gas (CNG) in the propulsion system of a road vehicle must after fitting be approved in compliance with the provisions of the regulations stated in the Appendix 6 of this regulation. Before the first use of the retrofit system the vehicle must be submitted to a final Executer/Executer nominated TPI acceptance to obtain a vehicle approval.
3- Special components for using compressed natural gas (CNG) in the propulsion system of a road vehicle must after fitting be approved in compliance with the provisions of the regulations stated in the Annex 1 of this regulation. Furthermore, for their installation, the conditions which are stated in the provisions of the regulations in the Appendix 6 of this regulation must be complied with. Before the first use of these components the vehicle must be submitted to a final Executer/Executer nominated TPI acceptance to obtain a vehicle approval.
4- Manufacturers of motor vehicles to Paragraph 1 or retrofit systems to Paragraph 2 or special components to paragraph 3 must include the required information documents corresponding to the requirements of the regulations stated in the Appendix 6, for fitting and safe use during the intended service life and the recommended maintenance. These documents must be made available when required to the persons responsible for fitting, operating and testing.
5- Vehicle owners, whose motor vehicles have been fitted with equipment to Paragraph 2 or Paragraph 3, after installation must submit their vehicles to a gas system installation test (GSIT) in accordance with Appendix 5(i). Gas system installation tests may only be carried out by:
a- Responsible persons of certified road vehicle workshops provided that the gas systems are installed at this road vehicle workshop
b- Governmentally authorized inspectors for road transport
After the gas system installation test the owners of motor vehicles with equipment to Paragraphs 2 and 3 must submit their vehicles to final Executer/Executer nominated TPI acceptance to obtain a new vehicle approval.
6- Owners whose road vehicles are fitted with equipment to Paragraphs 1 to 3 must undergo a gas system test (GST) to Appendix 5(i) at least every one year, or immendiately subsequent to any gas system repair. This also applies if the gas equipment is impaired by fire or accident. Gas system tests may only be carried out by:
a- Responsible persons of certified road vehicle workshops provided that gas systems are installed at this road vehicle workshop
b- Governmentally authorized inspectors for road transport
After the gas system test (GST) the owners of motor vehicles with equipment to Paragraphs 2 and 3 must submit their vehicles to final Executer/Executer nominated TPI acceptance.
7- The approval of road vehicle workshops for performing-the gas system installation tests to Paragraph 5, the gas system tests to Paragraph 6 is described in Appendix 5(ii). The training of the nominated persons according Paragraph 5 Clause b and Paragraph 2 and 3 as well as Paragraph 6 Clause c and Paragraph 2 and 3 has to be carried out in line with the requirements of numbers 2.5, and 7.3 of Appendix 5(ii)

II- Guidelines for carrying out gas system installation tests
(In the following abbreviated to GSIT) or the recurring or other gas system tests (in the following abbreviated to GST) (GSIT/GST-Guidelines for carrying out tests).م
1- General
1.1- The guidelines are valid for carrying out the GSIT to “I” Paragraph 5 and the GST to “I” Paragraph 6. GST to be made as a part of the annual vehicle re-inspection.
1.2- When carrying out the GSIT and the GST it is to be determined whether the condition and gas tightness of the tested vehicle are to the current state of the art and can be classified as “satisfactory”. To do so valid manufacturer information for the vehicle and the subsequent detailed scope of the work to be carried out.
1.3- As evidence of the determined condition of the gas system and its equipment for the inspected vehicle, a certificate is to be issued by the responsible person corresponding to the requirements of Appendix 5(i) No 2.4. An example of the evidence is given in the Form 1 of these guidelines. The evidence is to be kept for 3 years.
1.4- When testing the tightness of the gas system the leak detector used must be in accordance with the manufacturer’s directive.
2- Preparatory activities
2.1- Vehicle documentation to be presented.
2.2- Check that the documents presented belong to the vehicle concerned.
2.3- Determine for the examined vehicle which gas is used in order to ensure that the required equipment is available.
2.4- Ensure that the gas tank is at least 50% full with fuel or filled to the manufacturer’s requirements. If the quantity of gas is less than specified a further testing is not possible.
3- Carrying out the gas system test (GST)
3.1- Testing a gas (CNG) driven vehicle.
3.1.1- Identification of the components
By identifying the components it is ensured that only allowed and properly marked components were used.
For this are included:
- Gas tank
- Pressure regulator
- Gas pipe material
- Safety relevant components, e.g. non-return value, safety devices, receptacles, etc.
3.1.2- Visual inspection of components
All gas components are to undergo a visual inspection. During a visual inspection it is possible to check the condition (damage, corrosion and fixing) of the gas system without disassembly. To be carefully inspected
- Specified lifetime of the pressure gas container/s
- Receptacle
- Gas tank and fixation
- Tank amatures/ values
- Pressure relief device
- all gas conducting pipe work
- Gas vapor retaining systems
- Pressure regulator
- Injectors
- Warning lights/ control lights
3.1.3- Functional test
The function test includes:
- Main shut-off value of each gas tank
- Fuel chance-over switch-if fitted
- Control device/ start interruption
3.1.4- Leak test
A precondition for the leak test is that gas tank must be filled in compliance with number 2.4.
Set fuel change over switch to gas operation (only for bivalent operation) and start engine. With the engine running use the leak detector or the leak detection spray to carry out the leak test. During the test all screw connections, welded joints (if present), engine supply-pipes, valves and other relevant gas components are to be “sniffed” with the leak detector or sprayed with the leak detection spray.
If installed, chock also the high and low pressure components.
If when using the leak detector a gas emission is detected, then the leak detection spray is also to be used to localize the leaking connection or the peaking component. Possibly all un-tight connections and components are to be sprayed. The contact time period for the leak spay to become effective is at least 3 minutes. The gas system can be defined as leak free when within the effective period for the spray no bubbles are detected.
If it is not possible to undertake the test with the engine running, then the engine is to be run for at least 2 minutes under gas operation and after switching off the engine the leak test is to be carried out immediately.
The manufacturer’s information for carrying out the test is to be observed.
3.2- Issue of the certificate to No 1.3.
4- Carrying out the gas system installation test (GSIT)
4.1- Allocation of retrofit equipment to vehicle’s range of application
4.2- Allocation of workshop certification regarding the set-up and/or software version according to the provisions of the approval or the manufacturer
4.3- Installation inspection, e.g., installation of pipes/wires, labeling of pipes/wires, pipe/wiring connections, tank fixations, etc.
4.4- Functional test e.g. switching over from petrol to gas, shut-off valves etc..
4.5- Leak test of the system according to No 3.1.4
4.6- Issue of the certificate to No 1.3.


III- Guidelines for the approval of road vehicle workshops
To undertake gas system installation tests (in the following abbreviated to GSIT) or the recurring or other gas system tests (in the following abbreviated to GST) to I irt App I and II (Gaz workshop approval guidelines).
1 - General
These guidelines are valid for road vehicle workshops (in the following abbreviated to RVW), which carry out and certify GSIT or other GST as specified in “I” irt. Appendix I 5(i) and therefore need to be approved to Appendix II 5(ii) No 1. The certification to carry out the GSIT is also valid to carry out the GST. Only GSIT certified workshops are entitled and allowed to convert vehicles to use compressed natural gas (CNG) in there propulsion system according to paragraph (1) trough (3) of “I”.
2 - Application
The application for approval is to be sent to Executer according to Appendix 5(ii) No 1 in duplicate; it in cludes each operational workshop belonging to the applicant wanting to carry out the GSIT or the GST (Main address, branch offices(s) and associated companies). For each workshop a form is required as shown in Appendix 1 of these guidelines. The application forms are distributed by Executer./Executer nominated TPI.
2.1- along with the application the following documents are to be included,especially a certificate from the local chamber of trade/commerce the applicant in person or a person permanently employed by the RVW who is able to work indecently and possesses the required engineering skills to be able to correct any detected faults appertaining to a GSIT or GST,
2.2- a certificate of good conduct is required for presentation to the administration authority, and if required also for the nominated representatives as well as for the persons responsible for carrying out the GSIT or the GST. The information at the time of application may not be older than six months,
2.3- proof of the approval as “certified workshop” according to the least requirement as specified in 3.4.1 of these guidelines,
2.4- evidence that the person responsible for carrying out the GSIT or the GST as well as, if required, other employed specialists carrying out the GST have the necessary apprenticeship to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.4, evidence that the applicant or the persons responsible for carrying out the GSIT or the GST as well as, if required, other employed specialists carrying out the GST have undergone the necessary training to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.5 successfully.
2.5- confirmation of the required third party insurance to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.7- including an indemnity declaration to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.8.
3 - Requirements for certification
3.1 - Reliability
The applicant, its representatives according to the act, contract or by-laws and those responsible for carrying out the GSIT and the GST must be reliable people.
3.2 - Specialist knowledge
3.2.1- The applicant must prove that the specialist requirements to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.4 have been fulfilled, to clear detected deficiencies appertaining to the GSIT or the GST. This is not necessary if the applicant has appointed one or more responsible persons to carry out the GSIT or the GST
3.2.2- If the applicant has appointed one or more persons responsible for carrying out the GSIT or GST then the same specialist requirements have to be fulfilled to 3.2.1. The applicant is to give evidence of this. The responsible persons appointed by the applicant must be employed by him and work in the nominated workshop.
3.2.3- The applicant has to prove that the persons appointed to carry out the GSIT or the specialists appointed to carry out the GST have undergone the required training to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.5 successfully. With this the respective certificates of the required training are to be presented.
3.3 - Test location, Test and measurement devices and other equipment
3.3.1- The applicant must prove that each operating workshop which is to carry out the GSIT or the GST approved as a “certified conversion workshop”. For the continuing instruction of the persons carrying out the GSIT or the GST or those specialists carrying out the GST, the following latest versions of specified documents have to be provided and kept update:
3.3.1.1- Those for the GSIT relevant regulation of this ordinance including the applicable guidelines in the current valid edition,
3.3.1.2- Technical data and test instructions from the OEM or gas system manufacturer for carrying out the GSIT in the framework of the certification.
3.4 - Quality Assurance of the correct execution of the GSIT or the GST
3.4.1- Operating organization’s documentation
The RVW management must produce the documentation which contains the internal regulations that will guarantee the correct execution of the GSIT or the GST.
The documentation must as a minimum contain the requirements for:
- Representatives of the RVW to 3.4.2,
- Quality and equipment of operatingsites to 3.3,
- Qualifications and further training of staff which are involved with carrying out the GSIT or the GST to 3.2.3,
- Monitoring that the measuring and test equipment used comply with the relevant regulations for their calibration as well as maintenance instructions,
- Internal measures for the maintenance of quality of execution and documentation for the GSIT and the GST.
3.4.2- Representatives of the RVW
The RVW management appoint a representative (QA manager QAM) who within the organization is responsible for the measures to achieve the specified quality.
The QAM must
- have the qualifications to carry out the GST,
- report directly to the RVW management as long as he is not the management himself,
- the knowledge for implementing the regulations and guidelines for carrying out the GST as well as the operating organization’s acceptance procedure for the RVW,
- ensure that he is constantly informed of the current regulations and guidelines for carrying out the GSIT. If necessary he should undergo additional training. For the role of QAM and responsible person can also be nominated.
The QAM has in particular the following tasks:
- to check regularly the business organization and the operation of the RVW at his own responsibility for correlation of the relevant regulations with the RVW documentation,
- to make available on demand to Executer/Executer nominated TPI in coordination with the management of all required data, information and documentation,
- the checking of documentation to 3.4.1
- Compliance with the legal and, if required, internal requests are to be checked continuously by the RVW.
4 - Quality Assurance for execution of, and documentation for the GSIT or the GST
4.1 - Operating organization
The RVW has to explain for the application how it will ensure that it will adhere to the regulations to 3.4.1. After successful approval it is the duty of the RVW to enfore the regulations. The responsibility lies with the RVW management.
4.2 - Documentation of staff qualifications
The RVW will document without any omissions the following data and information for each specialist staff member:
- Training measures corresponding those published in the “GST/GSIT training guidelines”,
- Observance of possible executer collateral clauses.
The documentation is to be drawn up in duplicate; it must until the next examination by Executer/Executer nominated TPI be archived, but for at least 5 years.
4.3 - Evidence-Seal and stamp No
4.3.1- The stamp No, is allocated to the RVW by the Executer/Executer nominated TPI.
4.3.2- The proof seals of the type shown in Appendix 3 are obtained by the RVW from Executer/Executer nominated TPI and given as required to the responsible persons.
4.4 - Administration and use of proof seal and the embossing die
4.4.1- The RVW proves the correct administration and use of the individual proof seals continuously through a suitable procedure for a period of 3 years. Required for this are at least the following procedures:
- Purchase of the proof seal by the RVW,
- Application of the proof seal with correlation to the supporting documents,
- Disposition of the proof seal and the associated documents in special cases such as damage, destruction, theft and loss.
4.4.2- Suitable measures against theft and misuse if the proof seal and of stamp must be taken by the RVW. The precise instructions are to be defined in the company organization’s documentation to 3.4.1.
4.5- Documented evidence of conformity of executed GSIT or GST
4.5.1- The executed GSIT or GST are documented such that each time an up to date summary of the whereabouts of all supporting documents and proof seals is possible.
4.5.2- For all supporting documents to Appendix 5(ii) No 2.4, which are produced with the aid of an electronic, computer, a duplicate (copy) is to be archived for a period of 3 years.
4.5.3- Evaluation of executed GSIT or GST
- The QAM issues within a cycle of maximum 1 month for motor vehicles types which are fitted with special equipment or components for use with compressed natural gas (CNG) in their engine, standardized lists of each defect found during GSIT or GST, itemized for the actual test case and the determined deficiency. These lists (defect summaries) are to be kept for at least 3 years and presented on demand to Executer/Executer nominated TPI.
- Each document in evidence shall be made available within 2 working days including the information regarding official registration of the vehicle or
- the vehicle identification no. (at least the last 7 figures) in original form or as a copy of that to Executer/Executer nominated TPI.
4.6 - Test equipment monitoring
The RVW management guarantees by means of work and operating procedures that all measurement and test equipment used for GSIT and GST is functioning. Maintained to the manufacturer’s instructions, and in accordance with legal requirements tested and calibrated.
4.7 - Unexpected audits/ inspections
4.7.1- Executer/Executer nominated TPI are to check at least every 3 years compliance with the legal requirements and the provisions of these guidelines by the RVW. The requirement of the RVW is to make available all the required information and documentation. Deviations and breaches found can result in the withdrawal of the RVW’s license to carry out gas testing. In cases of dispute decision is taken by the jurisdiction.

5.1.1- Review of existing national UAE/ESMA CNG gas cylinders re-inspection guidelines/standards
Background: At present, there are a few regulations and codes of practice mandated by the UAE addressing directly or indirectly the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders.
ESMA has published several standards that are based on the United Nations ADR/RID also referring to ISO standards covering the re-inspection of gas cylinders:
- UAE.S GSO ISO 6406: Gas Cylinders - Seamless Steel Gas Cylinders - Periodic Inspection & Testing
- UAE.S GSO ISO 10460: Gas Cylinders - Welded Carbon Gas Cylinders Periodic Inspection & Testing
- UAE.S/GSO ISO 10461: Gas Cylinders - Seamless Aluminium-Alloy Gas Cylinders - Periodic Inspection And Testing
- UAE.S GSO ISO 11623: Transportable Gas Cylinders - Periodic Inspection & Testing of Composite Gas Cylinders
Observation: In February 2012, the Abu Dhabi EHS Center has released a Code of Practice “EHS RI-CoP 49.0 - Compressed Gases and Air” mainly serving as a guideline of how to use, store, and handle compressed gases valid for all employees dealing with compressed gases all over the UAE. Therein, paragraph 3.10 “Inspection and Maintenance” addresses the periodic inspection of these cylinders, but very generally and is lacking of respective details.
The UAE NGV manual already covers the requirement of periodic inspections of the CNG cylinders, referring to the UAE.S ECE R110 and manufacturer’s recommendations. However, to avoid contradictions, to keep updating simple, and not to include topics that should be dealt with separately, the NGV manual does not give specific and detailed instructions regarding the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders.
The present set of standards, the manual, and the Codes of Practice of above may serve a solid foundation for improving the Regulatory Framework for the periodic inspection and testing of automotive, transportable and stationary CNG cylinders.
Conclusion: There is no consistent set of local standards available for CNG cylinder re-inspection. However, there are three, which form part of ADR/RID (UAE.S GSO ISO 6406, UAE.S GSO ISO 10461, UAE.S GSO ISO 11623), and thus are a good choice.

5.1.2 - Review of existing international gas cylinders re-inspection guidelines/standards
5.1.2.1 - Stationary CNG Cylinders
Background: In the US, the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders is generally managed by the individual states, resulting in varying requirements for the Authorized Inspection Agencies including their Authorized Inspectors and the frequency and kind of inspection. These state specific laws and regulations are governed by the National Board Inspection Code (NBIS)-a 828 pages, three volumes document-developed by the National Board of Boiler and Pressure Vessel Inspectors covering all related topics like design, testing, repair, alternations, approval, training, etc. Since the NBIS is limited to ASME pressure equipment only, the respective detailed US states regulations cover such design solely as well. For the purpose of establishing a coherent and consistent re-inspection guideline in the UAE, the NBIS does not seem to fulfill these requirements.
Observation: In Asian and Chinese areas, the situation is comparable to the US, as the ASME standard is well established there and thus the national requirements for the periodic inspections of CNG cylinders are similar to and follow the NBIS approach to a great extent.
The European approach distinguishes two cases: the design and production of the products on the one hand, which is regulated by the European directives, norms and standards applicable all over Europe, and the operational requirements (which is with each single European country) on the other. Again, as this is an operational issue, the jurisdiction for specific requirements for the periodic inspection is with the national European member states and the associated countries. However, the contents of the national specifications are not completely liberal, as they have to follow general European requirements regarding occupational safety and health, laboratory requirements, inspectors’ qualifications, etc. resulting in a closed and consistent system of regulations, codes and standards. Moreover, to obtain general acceptance and recognition throughout Europe and the associated states, the relevant operational requirements are very similar to each other, with only minor differences in terms of legal procedures and responsibilities of the authorities (i.e. in Germany, the jurisdiction for operational issues is with the Ministry of Labor solely, whereas in Italy it is with both, the Ministry of Labor and the Ministry of Economy).
Conclusion: The UAE has already adopted and mandated the European directives regarding the manufacture of pressure equipment (i.e. PED = 97/23/EC) and whole systems (i.e. CNG fueling stations, UAE.S ISO 16923). This is why it seems to be a logical consequence of adopting the respective periodic inspection requirements as well.

5.1.2.2 - Transportable CNG Cylinders
Background: Since the UAE has found the UNECE’s ADR/RID (agreement concerning the carriage of dangerous goods on the road/by rail), which is a more specific and detailed version of the Model Regulations of the UN Recommendations on the Transport of Dangerous Goods, to be a proper regulatory framework to cover all specific means of designing, producing, testing, approving, and re-inspecting transportable pressure equipment, ADR/RID was adopted by the UAE to be followed as a reference. Additionally, to be coherent, the European TPED (Transportable Pressure Equipment) directive 2010/35/EU (formerly 1999/36/EC) was mandated, as the TPED as well as ASME may be used within the ADR/RID framework.
Observation: ADR/RID provides very detailed descriptions and/or references regarding the periodic inspection (not only) of CNG cylinders considering the different cylinder types 1 (all metal) through 4 (full composite) as well as battery vehicles and MEGC (both for bulk transport) including the absolute lifetime and re-inspection intervals. It is important to notice, that ADR/RID already includes detailed requirements for the periodic inspection and test body, and the respective approval system to be followed, describing the duties and responsibilities of the parties involved (authority, accreditation body, laboratory, etc.).
Conclusion: _Further to the periodic inspection standards already mandated by ESMA, ADR/RID also refers to the following gas cylinder re-inspection satandards in addition:
- EN 1968 Transportable gas cylinders. Periodic inspection and testing of seamless steel gas cylinders
- EN 1802-Transportable gas cylinders. Periodic inspection and testing of seamless aluminium alloy gas cylinders
- EN 1803 Transportable gas cylinders. Periodic inspection and testing of welded carbon steel gas cylinders
- UAE.S GSO ISO 22434 Transportable gas cylinders. Inspection and Maintenance of cylinder valves
UAE.S GSO ISO 11118 refers to design and type approval testing only
Remark:
In the USA, a comparable system is installed, which is governed by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration of the Department of Labor, and the Department of Transport.

5.1.2.3 - Automotive CNG Cylinders
Background: Scanning the relevant international sources of information reveal that all countries require a periodic external visual inspection of the CNG cylinders installed on-board a vehicle, but only a few demand an internal inspection or even a hydraulic test.
Observation: A reason to understand this approach might be found in the rather supporting experiences made in the past, when the internal inspections as well as the hydraulic test were mandatory resulting in three important conclusions:
a- Provided that the available gas quality is as per ISO 15403, and the cylinders are designed as per ISO 11439, NGV2, and/or ECE R110, the cylinder’s integrity is not affected and thus will not result in a reduced lifetime.
b- Even though oil (coalescence) filters are installed in CNG fuelling stations, there is always residual oil in the gas having the positive effect of protecting the internal shell of the NGV cylinders. An adverse influence on type 4 cylinders’ integrity may not be determined.
c- The hydraulic test is not suitable to assess type 4 cylinders’ residual strength and integrity.
Conclusion: Considering the findings of above, this may explain that the only referred to standard for a periodic inspection of CNG cylinders for automotive use is the ISO 19078 (or slightly amended and/or adopted versions, i.e. in the US), focusing on external inspections and integrity assessments. The present release of ISO 19078:2013 has waived the hydrostatic strength test for all cylinder types for reasons described therein in Annex D “Considerations on hydrostatic test and internal inspection”.

5.1.3 - Synthesis and recommendation for gas cylinders re-inspection guidelines and standards
Synthesis: This section refers to the synthesis only. The final procedure/process is to be found as a seperate procedure/guideline.
5.1.3.1 - Stationary CNG Cylinder
Recommendation: To maintain the present coherence and consistence of the applicable UAE pressure equipment related regulations (= European directives covering ASME as well), it seems to be advisable to follow the provisions of the respective European operational directives Directive 2009/104/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 16 September 2009 concerning the minimum safety and health requirements for the use of work equipment by workers at work, including the supporting directives 99/92/EC (ATEX) and 97/23/EC (PED). Doing so, this would result in the essential applicability of i.e. the respective German specification of the periodic inspection and testing of pressure equipment, including CNG cylinders, the TROS (Technical Rules Occupational Safety) No. 1201 “Technical Rules for Occupational Safety: Inspections due to risks of pressure”, the TROS No. 1203 “Technical Rules for Occupational Safety: Competent inspectors”, and accreditation requirements as per ISO 17025 “General requirements for the competence of testing and calibration laboratories” and TROS No. 1202” Test Laboratories for the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders: Requirements and Certification” describes the respective procedure and requirements for ISO 17025 compliance. If other testing facilities shall be used (i.e. manufacturer owned or operated by service providers), these tests have to be witnessed by an independent 3rd party which has to accredited as per ISO 17020 “Conformity assessment-- Requirements for the operation of various types of bodies performing inspection”, see TROS No. 1203 “Technical Rules for Occupational Safety: competent inspectors”.
5.1.3.2 - Transportable CNG Cylinders
Recommendation: Paragraph 5.1.2.2 proved already, that ADR/RID is a comprehensive and stringent regulatory framework covering the UAE requirements in terms of establishing a periodic inspection and testing procedure of CNG cylinders. Thus it is recommended to follow and to adopt the ADR/RID. The requirements of the ISO 17025 and/or ISO 17020 as per as per chapter 5.1.3.1 apply accordingly.
5.1.3.3 - Automotive CNG Cylinders
Recommendation: The relevant international regulations and standards do not require the hydraulic testing of automotive CNG cylinders anymore, for reasons given in paragraph 5.1.2.3. However, to gain own experience supporting the findings of this paragraph, it is suggested to implement the UAE.S GSO ISO 19078 for the visual inspection, and to use the hydraulic test requirements as per paragraph 5.1.3.1, stationary CNG cylinders. Additionally, following this approach, it must be agreed over the correct statistical number of cylinders to be pressure tested as a function of number of refills in total, cylinder age of use, and the cylinder type as these are critical parameters to be linked to each other and to be assessed carefully. The requirements of the UAE.S GSO ISO/IEC 17025 and/or UAE.S GSO ISO/IEC 17020 as per chapter 5.1.3.1 apply accordingly.
Appendix 8
Guideline for Re-Inspection of CNG Cylinders for Automative, Transportable and Stationary Use
1.1 - Purpose:
The purpose of this guideline is to provide systematic guidance for the periodic inspection, testing and certification of automotive, transportable and stationary CNG cylinders, including infrastructure requirements. This guideline is based on the report by the appointed Consultant for the requirements of comparable applicable international standards, while maintaining the present legal and regulatory framework of the UAE to avoid contradictions and non-coherences.
1.2 - Scope:
a- This Technical Guidelines for the periodic inspection and testing of automotive, transportable and stationary CNG cylinders shall be applicable to all new and existing cylinders in use.
b- This document highlights results of review of existing national UAE/ESMA gas cylinders re-inspection guidelines/standards in general including ESMA NGV regulation.
c- Considering the results of the study, development of a guideline for re-inspecting and retesting CNG cylinders (on site/in a lab) on a periodical basis was paramount. This guideline includes methods and procedures of how to inspect and test by use of suitable, appropriate existing international standards.
The following types of cylinders are manufactured and certified to:
- CNG cylinders installed in vehicles (UAE.S ECE R110)
- CNG cylinders installed as a static storage at CNG stations (PED 97/23/EC)
- CNG cylinders installed in CNG bulk trailers (ADR/TPED 2010/35/EU)
d- As a result, requirements for inspecting-testing facilities, including equipment, human resource and accessories are defined.

d- exothermic processes/reactions
e- intended lifetime (correlating with a)

Appendix 9
Technical Rule for Operational Safety TROS 1201 Inspections due to risks of pressure
1.1 - Purpose
Pressure equipment in the UAE, such as gas cylinders, are designed, produced and approved according to proven, internationally recognized standards ensuring a high level of safety for their use. However, during use, the cylinders’ integrity may be affected adversely for one reason or the other. Thus, to maintain safety, procedures need to be implemented describing how, when, and by whom pressure equipment shall be re-inspected and re-qualified.
1.2 - Scope
This Technical Rule for Operational Safety (TROS) specifies the requirements for the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders according to “Report on Re-Inspection Requirements of CNG Cylinders For Automotive, Transportable & Stationary Use” (Document no. TSA-CNG-R160902), chapters 5.1.3.1 “Stationary CNG cylinders”, 5.1.3.2 “Transportable CNG cylinders”, and 5.1.3.3. “Automotive CNG cylinders”. It addresses the inspection and testing of all pressure vessels excluding those with working pressures of less than 0.5 bar, and those falling within Article 3, paragraph 3 of 97/23/EC (PED).

1.8 - Method
1.8.1 - Types of Inspection
1.8.1.1 - Inspection of the state of CNG cylinders
The inspection of the state of the CNG cylinder forms part of the periodic inspection to assess, if the documentation provided is complete and consistent focusing on tests performed previously.
During the periodic inspection it shall be identified, if
- the type / design or the mode of operation has been changed since the last inspection (acc, to the statement of the operator),
- maintenance/repairs (acc, to the statement of the operator) were performed which could affect the safety of the CNG cylinder,
- all required documents are available (e,g, documentation of previous performed tests like inspection before commissioning, periodic inspection and special inspection under the responsibility of the operator),
- the requirements of the extent, scope and inspection intervals were defined and met.
If modifications at the CNG cylinder or at its attached equipment were made, it has to be demonstrated by an appropriate authenticated documentation.
1.8.1.2 - Overall Gas System Inspection
The purpose of the overall gas system inspection is the assessment of the general integrity of the CNG cylinder as well as the function of the safety related technical equipment of the cylinder. Testing will normally be performed during normal operation.
The overall gas system inspection covers for example:
- detection of vibration, leakages, discoloration, inacceptable accumulation of foreign/solid matters/debris, external corrosion, deformation of piping and equipment
- functional testing of safety related equipment/devices.
1.8.1.3 - External inspection
The external inspection covers the assessment of the external integrity of the cylinder and its safety related equipment. The presence, integrity and function of the required cylinders’ attached safety related equipment forms an essential part of the external inspection.
External inspection as a visual inspection may be replaced by different appropriate equivalent methods, if the visual inspection is not useful or applicable due to the design of the pressure equipment or due to the method of service. In general, the cylinder shall not be taken out of service for inspection. The acceptance criteria are given in chapter 1.8.3: Acceptance Criteria for Visual Inspection.
1.8.1.4 - Internal inspection
The purpose of the internal inspection is to assess the integrity of the cylinder’s shell. Doing so, the range depth of inspection should be based on potential deteriorations, while respective hints may be taken from the service manual of the manufacturer.
Generally, the internal inspection shall be performed as a visual assessment through the opening of the cylinder (cylinder valve removed), while additional appropriate inspection devices or alternative supporting test methods may be used; e.g. measuring of the wall thickness, detection of surface cracks. The cylinder or surrounding equipment shall be removed if the condition/integrity of the shell cannot be assessed satisfactorily, or if severe damage is suspected.
All internal inspections of the cylinders taken from a certain assembly (i.e. CNG fuelling station) shall be made before the re-inspection due date of the entire assembly.
Possible methods and procedures of internal inspection are in example:
- Visual inspection of the cylinders’ shells, if applicable with devices like mirrors, endoscopes, video camera systems
- Appropriate non-destructive testing, such as
• ultrasonic wall thickness measurement (spots or areas)
• surface crack detection (dye penetration testing or magnetic particle testing) to determine cracks
- ultrasonic or x-ray test methods to determine damages of the internal wall
The acceptance criteria are given in chapter 1.8.3: Acceptance Criteria for Visual Inspection.
1.8.1.5 - Strength testing
The purpose of the strength testing is the assessment of a sufficient strength of the shell compared to the allowed working pressure pw.
In general, the strength test shall be performed as a static pressure test with water or another appropriate liquid. During the overpressure testing, the cylinders will be checked for craks, inacceptable deformations or leakages.
If a liquid pressure test is not possible or not appropriate, the pressure test may be replaced by an aerostatic pressure test with gas considering safety while introducing specific protection measures. Alternatively, supporting non-destructive tests may be performed.
The test pressure ph is
- for hydraulic pressure testing: ph = 1.3x pw
- for gaseous pressure testing: ph = 1.1 x pw

Test Laboratories for the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders: Requirements and Certification
1.1 - Purpose
Pressure equipment in the UAE, such as gas cylinders, are designed, produced and approved according to proven, internationally recognized standards ensuring a high level of safety for their use. However, during use, the cylinders’ integrity may be affected adversely for one reason or the other. Thus, to maintain safety, procedures need to be implemented describing how, when, and by whom pressure equipment shall be re-inspected and re-qualified.
1.2 - Scope
This Technical Rule for Operational Safety (TROS) specifies the requirements, tasks and duties of test labs for the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders according to “Report on Re-Inspection Requirements of CNG Cylinders for Automotive, Transportable & Stationary Use” (Document no TSA-CNG-R160902 chapters’ 5.1.3.1 “Stationary CNG cylinders”, 5.1.3.2 “Transportable CNG cylinders”, and 5.1.3.3. “Automotive CNG cylinders”.

The respective College and University Degrees in mechanical engineering or natural sciences are considered to be equal to certified technical apprenticeship/technical professional trainings at least. 1.8.3 - Professional Experience
A basic prerequisite for professional experience is evidence, proving that the designated competent inspector has used and/or dealt with CNG cylinders for two years at least, knowing their technologies, operational modes, and risks to a sufficient extent. Additionally, the applicant shall provide evidence showing that he has witnessed respective periodic inspections, and knows how to use the test equipment and how to interpret test results. Professional experience includes, but is not limited to the capability of assessing the state of a test set-up and its general safety. A continuous further education is essential.
1.8.4 - Present Professional Work
The competent inspector shall be employed by an approved CNG cylinders test laboratory as per TROS 1202 “Test Laboratories for the periodic inspection of CNG cylinders: Requirements and Certification”.
An acceptable present professional work shall cover on-going respective inspection tasks and a continuous further education. A reasonable number of annual inspections, but not less than 22 per year, shall be proven to maintain the practical experience obtained.
In case of a discontinuation of the inspection tasks exceeding 6 months, the competent inspector shall witness not less than two (2) tests per discontinued month to renew the skills before resuming the inspections at his own responsibility.
A continuous further education is essential addressing the state of the art of CNG cylinder testing, Occupational Safety and Health, risk assessment, etc., see chapter 1.8.6/1.8.7.4

- Safety Assessments
- Impacts affecting the cylinders’ integrity, failure modes, etc.

A basic prerequisite for professional experience is an evidence proving that the designated accredited inspector has used and/or dealt with CNG cylinders for two years at least, knowing their technologies, operational modes, and risks to a sufficient extent. Additionally, the applicant shall provide evidence showing that he has witnessed respective periodic inspections, and knows how to use the test equipment and how to interpret test results. Professional experience includes, but is not limited to the capability of assessing the state of a test set-up and its general safety. A continuous further education is essential.
1.8.10 - Present Professional Work
The accredited inspector shall be employed by an independent 3rd party organization, accredited by ESMA for the respective tasks.
An acceptable present professional work shall cover on-going respective inspection tasks and a continuous further education. A reasonable number of annual inspections, but not less than 22 per year, shall be proven to maintain the pratical experience obtained.
In case of a discontinuation of the inspection tasks exceeding 6 months, the competent inspector shall witness not less than two (2) tests per discontinued month to renew the skills before resuming the inspections at his own responsibility.
A continuous further education is essential addressing the state of the art of CNG cylinder testing, Occupational Safety and Health, risk assessment, etc., see chapter 1.8.6
1.8.11 - Specific Requirements
The Accredited Inspector shall have the following knowledge and skills, which shall be maintained to adequate measures:
- Re-Inspection Guideline for “CNG cylinders for automotive, transportable, and stationary use”
- Applicable UAE regulations, standards and technical rules, such as UAE.S ECE R110, UAE.S ECE R115, UAE.S 5006, OSHA regulations, PED (97/23/EC), TROS 1201
- Material Sciences in conjunction with calculation methods focusing on fatigue
- Safety Assessments
- Impacts affecting the cylinders’ integrity, failure modes, etc.
1.8.12 - Legal Formalities
The applicant shall apply officially with ESMA/Executer to obtain the approval as a competent inspector, supported by the following documents:
- Application letter
- CV
- Certificate(s) of technical apprenticeship(s) technical professional training
- Certificates of related trainings proving the requirements of chapter 1.8.6/1.8.7.4
- Job references
For the approval, ESMA’s accreditation procedures apply.
1.8.12.1 - Assessment and certification
For competent inspectors, Executer will review and verify the provided documents as per chapter 1.8.7.5, and decide eventually, if the requirements of this TROS are met. The applicant will be informed respectively, and in case of a positive result, obtain a certificate allowing him to inspect stationary and automotive CNG cylinders based on the provisions made in Executer’s Re-Inspection Guideline “CNG cylinders for automotive, transportable, and stationary use”.
The certificate is valid for three years, and is subject to an extension based on the procedure of this TROS.
Accredited inspectors are deemed to meet the requirements of above. An additional certificate will not be issued.
