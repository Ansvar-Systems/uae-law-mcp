Directive (4) of 2019
Regarding the Mandatory Introduction of a CAP Limit on the purchase of Additional Services
Version: 1.0
Issue Date: 24 Sep. 2019
1- DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION
1.1 The terms, words, and phrases used in this Directive shall have the same meaning as are ascribed to them in the Telecommunications Law, unless this Directive expressly provides otherwise, or the context in which those terms, words and phrases are used in this Directive requires otherwise. For the purposes of this Directive, the following terms and words shall have the meanings ascribed to them below:
1.1.1 “Additional Service” as defined in the CPR means - any optional non-telecommunications service supplied by a Partner or a Licensee including but not limited to: games, content, apps, ringtones, third-party products, third-party services, etc. and where the fees, levies, or charges for such extra service(s) are collected by the concerned Licensee via the Subscriber’s account
1.1.2 “Additional Service Purchase” means - any purchase of an Additional Service made by a Consumer, over and above contracted telecommunications service(s), where the purchase price for such purchase is collected by a Licensee through a post-paid invoice, or by a deduction from a Subscriber’s pre-paid credit balance. See section 5.1 of this Directive for clarifications and exemptions.
1.1.3 “CAP” means - a Subscriber selectable upper limit on the total amount of money, measured in Dirhams (AED), which can be invoiced or billed by a Licensee in relation to any Additional Services Purchases made in any one month.
1.1.4 “Consumer Protection Regulations” or “CPR” means - the Consumer Protection Regulations, Version 1.4, Issued 20 March 2019.
1.1.5 “EITC” means - Emirates Integrated Telecommunications Company and its successors.
1.1.6 “EITC Licence” means - Public Telecommunications Licence No. (2) of 2006 as granted to EITC.
1.1.7 “Etisalat” means - Etisalat Telecommunications Group and its successors.
1.1.8 “Etisalat Licence” means - Public Telecommunications Licence No. (1) of 2006 as granted to Etisalat.
1.1.9 “Executive Order” - means Decision of the Supreme Committee for the Supervision of the Telecommunications Sector No. (3) of 2004 Issuing the Executive Order of Federal Law by Decrees No (3) of the 2003 Regarding the Organisation of the Telecommunications Sector.
1.1.10 “Extended Mandatory Instructions” - means the letters sent on 30 April 2017 to each of EITC and Etisalat under the subject of ‘Extended Mandatory Instructions Regarding Subscriptions to Third Party Applications” and having the reference number TRA/RA/17/1265 for EITC, and TRA/RA/17/1255 for Etisalat.
1.1.11 “Implementation Date” means - the date, as specified in Article 8.2, by which Licensees shall fully implement this Directive.
1.1.12 “Licensee” means - Etisalat or EITC as appropriate and includes all ‘brands’ used by Etisalat to provide service under the Etisalat Licence and all 'brands’ used by EITC to provide services under the EITC Licence.
1.1.13 “Mandatory Instructions” - means the letters sent on 1 February 2017 to each of EITC and Etisalat under the subject of “Mandatory Instructions Regarding Subscriptions to Third Party Applications” and having the reference number TRA/RA/17/1084 for EITC, and TRA/RA/17/1076 for Etisalat.
1.1.14 “OTP” or “One-time Password” - means an automatically generated numeric or alphanumeric string of characters that has a limited-life and authenticates the user for a single transaction or session only.
1.1.15 “Regulatory Framework” means - the complete body of resolutions, licences, authorisations, policies, regulations, instructions, directives (including this Directive), orders, determinations, reports, procedures, guidelines, and other such regulatory instruments as issued and as may be issued by the TRA and as may be amended from time-to-time.
1.1.16 “Telecommunications Law” - means Federal Law by Decree No.3 of 2003 Regarding the Organisation of the Telecommunications Sector, as amended.
1.1.17 “TRA” or “Telecommunications Regulatory Authority” - means the General Authority for Regulating the Telecommunications Sector as established by Article 6 of the Telecommunications Law.
1.2 In interpreting the provisions of this Directive, unless this Directive expressly
provides otherwise, or the context requires otherwise:
1.2.1 words importing one gender shall be construed as importing all genders;
1.2.2 words importing the singular shall be construed as importing the plural and vice-versa;
1.2.3 references to articles, sub-articles, annexure, and attachments shall be construed as being references to articles, sub-articles, annexure, and attachments in or to this Directive;
1.2.4 headings and sub-headings in this Directive are intended for guidance and shall not be construed as limiting the effect of the provisions of this Directive.
1.3 This Directive may be referred to by the short name- the “CAP Limit Directive”.
2- INTRODUCTION
2.1 It has become common for Consumers to make Additional Service Purchases via their mobile phones or/and other devices, such as TV remote control units. Such Additional Purchases usually relate to third-party Apps, ringtones, games, music, videos, in-App purchases, etc. but can equally be related to Licensee supplied Apps, ringtones, games, music, videos, in-App purchases, etc.
2.2 The wide proliferation of smartphones, smart -TVs, tablets, computers, on-line gaming consoles etc., has made it very easy for Consumers to make Additional Service Purchases. In fact, at one point it was so easy for Consumers to make Additional Service Purchases it was possible for Consumers to make such purchases by accident. Naturally, those circumstances gave rise to a significant volume of complaints by Consumers regarding disputed purchases and/or excessive invoice amounts.
2.3 Acting in the interests of Consumers, with a view to promoting Consumer welfare by eradicating the cause of such complaints, the TRA has intervened on several occasions. The TRA’s interventions have included: amendments to the Consumer Protection Regulations, issuing the Mandatory Instructions, and issuing the Extended Mandatory Instructions.
2.4 Despite the TRA’s significant interventions and efforts, the TRA continues to receive complaints regarding bill shock arising from excessive invoices. The TRA is greatly alarmed by this situation. Accordingly, the TRA considers that further intervention is required in order to safeguard the interests of Consumers and to promote customer happiness and welfare and to strengthen Consumer trust in the telecommunications sector. To that end, the TRA is issuing this Directive to obligate Licensees to implement a monthly CAP limit on Additional Service Purchases as set out herein.
3- LEGAL BACKGROUND
3.1 Article 12 of the Telecommunications Law establishes the TRA as the competent body to oversee the telecommunications sector in the State and all Licensees in accordance with the Telecommunications Law, the Executive Order, and the instructions of the Board.
3.2 Article 13(2) of the Telecommunications Law provides that the TRA “shall exercise its functions and powers... to... enhance the level of service provided by the telecommunications sector in order to promote the interests of subscribers”.
3.3 Article 14(3) of the Telecommunications Law grants the TRA with the powers to “issue regulations, instructions, decisions and rules regulating... the conditions, level and scope of services provided by the Licensees to subscribers... including the... terms of supply”.
3.4 EITC and Etisalat each provide Public Telecommunications Services in the UAE pursuant to the provisions of the EITC Licence and the Etisalat Licence respectively. Article 8.2 of each of the EITC Licence and the Etisalat Licence states: “The Licensee shall comply with any directions as the TRA or other competent authority may issue from time to time on matters relating to public interest, safety and/or national security...”.
3.5 The TRA submits that it is in the public interest for Consumers to be protected against accidental Additional Purchases and/or related bill shock.
4- THE DIRECTIVE
4.1 Having considered:
(a) the provisions of the Telecommunications Law, it’s Executive Order and the Regulatory Framework; and
(b) acting in the interests of protecting the welfare of Consumers; the TRA issues this Directive to all Licensees.
4.2 By no later than the Implementation Date, Licensees shall:
4.2.1 adopt and implement robust business processes to ensure full compliance with this Directive;
4.2.2 inform all Subscribers of the operation of the CAP and advise Subscribers of the procedure by which Subscribers may select and set a CAP at an amount of the Subscriber’s choosing;
4.2.3 impose and strictly enforce a CAP on the purchase of Additional Services. The CAP shall, by default, be set at AED 0 per month which means that only those Subscribers who take specific actions in accordance with this Directive will be able to make Additional Services Purchases.
4.2.4 Develop and implement a mechanism whereby each Subscriber can either select a personal CAP, or if the Subscriber prefers to set the CAP as being unlimited.
5- SCOPE
5.1 This Directive applies to all Additional Service Purchases- that is all purchases and/or subscriptions made on top off of contracted telecommunications services, where the fees related to such purchases are collected by a Licensee either as a listed item on a post-paid account, or by deduction of funds from a pre-paid credit balance regardless of whether the post-paid or pre-paid account relates to mobile or fixed telecommunications services, and regardless of the device or devices used by the Consumer to make the Additional Purchase, and regardless of whether the Additional Services are supplied by Licensees or by third-parties.
5.2 For the avoidance of doubt, this Directive does not apply to any purchase where the fees related to the purchase in question are contemporaneously settled by some arrangement outside of the Licensee’s billing procedures such as by direct payment made at the time of the purchase.
6- LICENSEE APPs
6.1 Licensees are encouraged to develop smart functionality on Licensee’s portals including Apps and websites etc. whereby Subscribers can use the portals to:
6.1.1 Individually manage their CAP limits, in order to set, increase, and/or decrease the monetary amount of their CAP limits;
6.1.2 track Additional Services Purchases and view the history of such purchases, etc. etc.
7- RESOLUTION OF CONSUMER DISPUTES
7.1 If a Consumer has a complaint regarding an excessive amount being invoiced (post-paid account) or billed (pre-paid account) in relation to Additional Services Purchases, the TRA will expect the concerned Licensee to provide evidence that the Subscriber has adjusted his/her default CAP and has set an individual CAP at a monetary amount higher than that of the disputed invoiced amount for all Additional Services Purchases in the month in question.
7.2 In the event that the concerned Licensee cannot produce satisfactory evidence as described in 7.1 above, the TRA will instruct that Licensee to reimburse the Subscriber for the whole of the disputed amount.
8- EFFECTIVE DATE AND IMPLEMENTATION DATE
8.1 This Directive shall become effective as of the date of issue.
8.2 The Implementation Date shall be 1 November 2019.
8.3 This Directive shall remain in force until otherwise determined by the TRA.
9- ACKNOWLEDGEMENT AND COMPLIANCE
9.1 The Licensees shall notify the TRA in writing of receipt of this Directive within one (1) business day of the date it is received.
--- End of Directive ---

[1] The language “invoiced or billed” is intended to cover amounts invoiced by Licensees through post-paid invoices, as well as amounts deducted by Licensees from pre-paid credit balances. Thus, the CAP applies to pre-paid and post-paid accounts.
[2] In circumstances where Licensees allow Subscribers to select a CAP from a list of predetermined amounts, the extent of the list shall be sufficiently broad to allow ‘low volume users’ to select a relatively small CAP - e g. AED 50 per month Thus, it will not be permitted for Licensees to offer a choice along the lines of- ‘Please select one of the following CAPs: AED 10,000, AED 20,000, AED 30,000’...
[3] The provisions of this Article 6 are recommendations and illustrations only and do not form a mandatory part of this Directive.
