1. Background
1.1 Given:
the progress of the Industry Group for Number Portability ("IGNP");
the agreement of members of the IGNP to use All Call Query (ACQ) as a technical solution for Number Portability;
the issue of a Request for Proposal by the TRA (on behalf of the IGNP) to potential suppliers of a Mobile Number Porting System (the "RFP");
the decision of the IGNP as to the most appropriate vendor of a Mobile Number Porting System, based upon responses to the RFP received;
the express commitment of each of Etisalat and du to pay for the establishment and operation of a Mobile Number Porting System with a system availability of 99.9% and the ability to carry out 150,000 ports annually;
the powers and obligations of the TRA under the Federal Law by Decree no 3 of 2003 regarding the Organization of the Telecommunication Sector
the TRA has decided that it is appropriate to make these Instructions with respect to Mobile Number Portability. These Instructions are in addition to, not in substitution of the TRA's Policy for Number Portability.
2. Legal Reference
2.1 Article 14(8) of Federal Law by Decree no 3 of 2003 regarding the Organization of the Telecommunication Sector grants the TRA the power, inter alia, to issue regulations, orders, resolutions and procedures in relation to Mobile Number Portability.
2.2. The TRA has issued the TRA's Policy for Number Portability.
3. Definitions:
3.1 All words, terms and phrases used in these Instructions shall have the meanings ascribed to them, if any, in either the Federal Law by Decree no 3 of 2003 regarding the Organization of the Telecommunication Sector, the TRA's Policy for Number Portability, or the TRA's Policy for National Numbering Plan, in each case as amended or replaced from time to time as the case may be, unless otherwise provided for in these Instructions.
3.2 For the purposes of these Instructions, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them immediately below:
Service Commencement Date The date which the TRA designates as the date on which Licensees shall provide Mobile Number Portability to Subscribers.
RFP Has the meaning ascribed to it in paragraph 1.1.
IGNP Has the meaning ascribed to it in paragraph 1.1.
Licensees Emirates Telecommunications Corporation (Etisalat) and Emirates Integrated Telecommunication Company (du).
MNP Contract The Development and Managed Services Agreement between the MNP Provider and the TRA for the provision and operation of Mobile Number Porting System so as to enable MNP to be provided to Subscribers in the UAE.
MNP Provider The entity appointed to build and operate the Mobile Number Porting System under the MNP Contract including all associated hardware, software, communications systems and other infrastructure items.
Mobile Number Portability or MNP The capability or service whereby a Subscriber who is being provided with a Telecommunications Service for mobile communications services in association or connection with a particular Mobile Number, can terminate the contract with the Licensee providing that Telecommunications Service, but continue to use that Mobile Number in association or connection with the provision of a Telecommunications Service for mobile communications services by another Licensee.
New Licensees An entity licensed to provide Telecommunication Services in the UAE after the coming into force of these Instructions.
Port With respect to a specific Number, the retention by the relevant Subscriber of the use of that Number while following a change in the identity of the Licensee providing the relevant Telecommunications Service, and the terms "Porting", "Ported" and "Porting Process" shall all have corresponding meanings.
Prepaid Subscriber A Subscriber for Telecommunications Service where the relevant Licensee allows the Subscriber to pay for the Telecommunications Service in advance of the actual use of the Telecommunications Services.
Postpaid Subscriber A Subscriber for Telecommunications Service where the relevant Licensee allows the Subscriber to pay for a significant portion of the Telecommunications Services subsequent to the actual use of the Telecommunications Service.
Porting Process Specification The detailed set of specifications describing the process for the provision of Mobile Number Portability in the UAE, set out as Annex 1 to these Instructions, or as may be published by the TRA from time to time.
MNP Regulatory Fees The fees and other charges imposed from time to time by the TRA on the Licensees (and if appropriate, on any New Licensees, or on any of them), referred to in paragraph 4.6.
4. Instructions
4.1 The Licensees shall implement MNP in their respective networks and systems, and make it commercially available to their respective Subscribers, in accordance with the Regulatory Framework, by no later than the Service Commencement Date.
4.2 Licensees shall fully co-operate with other Licensees, the MNP Provider, and the TRA in order to ensure that MNP is commercially available to each of the Licensee's Subscribers by the Service Commencement Date.
4.3 The Licensees are obliged to run a national advertising campaign concerning the availability of Mobile Number Portability in the UAE, to the satisfaction of the TRA.
4.4 Licensees shall not, nor shall they attempt to interfere, restrict, prohibit, delay or frustrate the attempts of the MNP Provider to carry out its contractual obligations to the TRA under the MNP Contract.
4.5 With relation to the costs associated with the implementation of Mobile Number Portability, the following shall apply:
4.5.1 the Licensees shall bear all of their own setup and maintenance costs in relation to interfaces with the Centralized Data Base, their Mobile Number portability management team and all other internal and external costs associated with the preparation of their networks for the introduction and provision of Mobile Number Portability;
4.5.2 the Licensees shall bear all costs associated with the establishment and operation of the Mobile Number Porting System (PROVIDED THAT the TRA may provide otherwise, where for example, any New Licensees become subject to these Instructions);
4.5.3 the costs associated with the establishment and operation of the Mobile Number Porting System shall be apportioned equally between the Licensees, and may be recovered by the TRA from the Licensees in accordance with the Regulatory Framework in place from time to time;
4.5.4 the Licensees shall bear their own additional communication costs and database query costs incurred in relation to the provision of Telecommunication Services to Subscribers who have had the relevant Mobile Numbers Ported;
4.5.5 the Donor Licensee shall not be entitled to recover any of its costs incurred in relation to Number Portability from the Recipient Licensee, and nor shall the Recipient Licensee be entitled to recover any of its costs from the Donor Licensee; and
4.5.6 the Donor Licensee shall not be entitled to recover from the relevant Subscriber, any of its costs associated with the Porting of a Number. For the avoidance of doubt, nothing in these instructions will deny the Donor Licensee the right to enforce any contractual rights that it may have against a Subscriber, for early termination of a contract for the provision of Telecommunications Services PROVIDED THAT the Donor Licensee does not thereby recover or seek to recover any costs associated with the Porting of the relevant Number.
4.6 The TRA shall from time to time, in its absolute discretion either
4.6.1 recover from the Licensees (and if applicable, from any New Licensees); or
4.6.2 require payment in advance from the Licensees (and if applicable, from any New Licensees),
the amount of any costs, charges, fees and expenses incurred directly or indirectly by the TRA as a result of the introduction of MNP into the UAE, including any costs, charges, fees, and expenses incurred by the TRA under the MNP Contract. The TRA may do this through any mechanism it sees fit, including but not limited to the imposition of MNP Regulatory Fees.
The Licensees (and if applicable, any New Licensees) shall pay any MNP Regulatory Fees strictly as provided for by the TRA.
4.7 Unless specified otherwise by the TRA in writing, the time taken between a request to port a Mobile Number being received and the commencement of the provision of service on the Recipient Licensee's network should be less than one (1) working day.
4.8 The Donor Licensee shall only refuse to accept a porting request from a Subscriber for the reasons set out in the Porting Process Specification.
4.9 It is the responsibility of the Recipient Licensee to take all reasonable steps to ensure that any eligibility requirements for Subscribers to be able to Port Mobile Numbers (which eligibility requirements may be notified by the TRA to the Licensees) are met.
4.10. Prepaid Subscribers shall be able to Port without any restriction related to the settlement of any debts owed to the Donor Licensee by the Prepaid Subscriber. Furthermore, the Donor Licensee shall not reject Porting requests solely on the basis that the Postpaid Subscriber has outstanding debt PROVIDED THAT they are not subject to any kind of service suspension due to unpaid bills related to the Number to be Ported.
4.11. Postpaid Subscribers shall be able, during the Porting process, to receive all free of charge services, to make and receive all calls to geographic and Mobile Numbers within the UAE and international destinations, including national and international SMS. However, Licensees are not required to make Shared Revenue Services available to Postpaid Subscribers during the Porting process.
4.12 There shall be no limitation on the service features available to Prepaid Subscribers by either the Donor Licensee or the Recipient Licensee, as appropriate, as a result of the Porting process PROVIDED THAT:
a. those Prepaid Subscribers have sufficient credit with the relevant Licensee at the relevant time; and
b. in the case of international roaming services, the relevant Licensee is able to apply instant billing/deduction from the Prepaid Subscriber's credit.
4.13 The Donor Licensee shall not use information gained from Number Portability for marketing purposes, or to contact Subscribers or potential Subscribers, whether in relation to sales and marketing strategies including to those referred to as "retention" or "winback".
4.14 The Licensees shall report the following to the TRA at periods determined by the TRA:
4.15 1 the number of requests Recipient Licensees receive for the porting of individual telephone numbers or telephone number groups;
4.15.2 the number of requests for porting made by the Recipient Licensee that have been ejected by the Donor Licensee, including the reasons for rejection;
4.14.3 the number of ports which were not effected within the time scales agreed between the Licensees and approved by the TRA; and
4.15.4 any other requests for information that the TRA may make from time to time,

1. Introduction
1.1 All words, terms and phrases used in these Instructions shall have the meanings ascribed to them, if any, in either the Federal Law by Decree no 3 of 2003 regarding the Organization of the Telecommunication Sector, the TRA's Policy for Number Portability, or the TRA's Policy for National Numbering Plan, in each case as amended or replaced from time to time as the case may be, unless otherwise provided for in these Instructions,
1.2 The purpose of this document is to define the Porting Process for Mobile Number Portability in UAE.
1.3 This document refers only to Mobile Number Portability, and does not cover fixed Number Portability, or any other form of Number or service portability.
1.4 Without limitation to anything else provided for in this document, the National Porting System shall allow the following Porting features:
- “Change,” allowing the RL to change information in the original Porting Request; and
- "Cancel," which allows the RL to cancel the Porting Request.
The above features shall be made available with the option to enable or disable them.
1.5 The National Porting System shall utilize easily configurable timers to identify constraints that govern the order of execution of the Porting Processes, and help ensure that Porting Requests are implemented in a timely manner. Different timer values may be defined for each of the timers according to such characteristics as installation type, or number-type. It shall be possible to configure and change timing values at any time throughout the operation of National Porting System.
1.6 The National Porting System shall acknowledge each message received from either the DL or the RL. Such acknowledgement messages shall be sent by the National Porting System within one minute of receipt by the National Porting System of the message to which that acknowledgement message relates.
1.7 The National Porting System shall record all transactions/messages set out in this document.
2. Rules for Porting Numbers
The following rules shall apply for Porting Numbers between Licensees.
2.1 MNP will be available to both pre-paid and post-paid subscribers.
2.2 Numbering resources should be used in accordance with the National Numbering Plan Policy.
2.3 To activate a Porting Request, the PS is required to contact only the RL, i.e. "one-stop shop' process.
2.4 The RL and DL should implement the appropriate technical capabilities within their respective networks to ensure that both Ported and non-Ported customers can subscribe equally to the same set of services within any one network.
2.5 All valid Porting Requests will be carried out fairly and transparently by the Licensees.
2.6 All valid Porting Requests received by the DL will be handled by the DL on a first-come-first-served principle based on the "date/time", as time-stamped by the National Porting System.
2.7 The DL shall not use any information specifically obtained during the Porting Process to endeavour to persuade the PS to cancel or delay the Application,
2.8 Porting Requests will only be addressed on the direct request of an account holding subscriber or an authorized agent of the subscriber, authorised by and acting on behalf of the account holding subscriber, to the RL. Where a PS or an authorised person fails to provide adequate identification of himself to the RL (as specified in Annex 6), then the Application shall be refused by the RL.
2.9 If the RL fails to make adequate checks but still proceeds with the Porting Request then the RL shall bear the responsibility of that decision. If it subsequently proves to be the case that the PS or an authorised person acting on behalf of the PS did not make the original Porting Request (i.e. the PS has been 'slammed') then it is the responsibility of the RL to arrange restoration of the PS to the DL, and the RL will be liable for any subsequent claims arising thereof.
2.10 Pre-paid PS: Any pre-paid PS shall be able to Port without any restriction related to the settlement of debts,
2.11 Post-paid PS: DL shall not reject Porting Requests solely on the basis that the post-paid PS has outstanding debt provided that the post-paid PS is not subject to any kind of service suspension due to unpaid bills related to the Porting Number at Porting Request receipt by the DL.
2.12 For the avoidance of doubt, the DL does have the right to reject a Porting Request for a post-paid PS who is subject to suspension of incoming and/or outgoing calls due to unpaid bills (related to the Porting Number) outside of the normal bill payment timescales.
2.13 Post-paid PS shall be able, during the Porting Process, to make and receive all free of charge services (i.e. receiving all calls, SMS, MMS, and dialling free of charge calls) without any limitation.
2.14 Post-paid PS shall be able, during the Porting Process, to make all calls to mobile and geographic numbers within the UAE and international destinations, including national and international SMS. However, shared revenue services are not necessarily required to be available to post-paid PS during the Porting Process.
2.15 In cases of service limitations for shared revenue services and for any sort of usage in relation to post-paid PS, the limitation thereof shall not exceed one (1) Working Day, even if the actual Porting Process takes longer time. The DL shall be held accountable for failure to remove these limitations.
2.16 There shall be no service limitation applied to a pre-paid PS as a result of the Porting Process provided that those subscribers have available credit. However, the TRA will allow an exception for international roaming services if the DL is not able to apply instant billing/deduction from the PS's credit.
2.17 Any remaining credit on the accounts of pre-paid PS will not be transferred with the Porting Request to the PS account with RL when a Porting Request is accepted. Rather, the PS must be advised that any such credit may be lost unless used (i.e. "use it or lose it") in line with the normal terms & conditions of a "service termination" of the DL.
2.18 Any credit or balance remaining on a post-paid PS's account will similarly not be transferred with the Port, but will be assessed by the DL on a case by case basis and subject to the PS's contract with the DL and/or refund policies of the DL.
2.19 Porting of a primary Number only will result in that PS will lose all the secondary Numbers associated with the primary Number. The PS should be notified of this fact.
2.20 Porting of a primary Number and the associated secondary Number(s), shall be sent within the same Porting form request.
2.21 Porting of a secondary Number only is not allowed, unless it is converted to a primary Number first.
2.22 A valid Porting Request sent from the RL to the DL will include a specific form/statement for terminating the contract for the relevant Telecommunications Service associated with the Ported Number with the DL and must be signed by the PS or an authorized agent of the PS.
2.23 A Porting Request can refer to a single Number or to multiple Numbers held by the same PS.
2.24 Porting Requests for a block of less than 10 sequential Numbers shall be handled as individual Porting Requests for each Number.
2.25 Porting Requests for a block of 10 or more sequential Numbers shall be handled as a Block-Porting Request. It is to be recognised that, whilst it does take longer to Port (for example) one hundred sequential Numbers than it takes to Port a single Number, it does not take one hundred times as long. Such Block-Porting Request will be considered as equivalent to 10 individual Porting Requests and RL should avoid submitting more than one Block- Porting Request in one Working Day.
2.26 The processes between the initial Port Request and Port activation time shall be done within one Working Day.
2.27 Where the PS requests a Porting date that exceeds the one working day period, then the RL can issue a "Pro-Order Porting Request" to the DL. (see Annex 3). The Pre-Order Porting Request shall be subject to the same checks by the DL as though it was an ordinary Porting Request and the DL will issue a "Pre-Order Porting Request Accept" (or Reject) notification. Submitting the Pre-Order Porting Request does not change the fact that in order for the Porting to proceed, the RL must submit a Porting Request to the DL for performing the Porting of the Porting Number on the Porting date suitable to the PS. It is understood that Licensee's Porting daily capacity for each "Pre-Order Porting Request" will be used twice, i.e. at the day of Pre- Order Porting Request and at the actual Porting day. The actual Porting will be subject to further verifications by DL at actual Porting time and should conditions of Pre-Order Porting Request Accept have changed, then Porting may be rejected by the DL.
2.28 A request to Port back a single Number should not be refused by the current RL If it is within 3 (three) Working Days of a previously activated Port in respect of that Number. In addition, and except for the case of above Port back, in order to prevent abuse of the Porting capability either Licensee is permitted, at their discretion, to refuse to Port a Number more than one time in any 30 (thirty) day period.
3. Process description
3.1 Background
3.1.1 The Porting Process shall be split into three phases: Negotiation Phase, Provisioning Phase, and Completion Phase as described below. The workflow of the Porting Process is described in Annex 2.
3.1.2 Unless provided in this Porting Process Specification otherwise, all communications during this Porting Process will be carried via the National Porting System.
3.1.3 The transfer of information during the Porting Process shall be fully electronic. However, the overall Porting Process may require manual intervention at certain stages e.g. documents may require to be scanned; photographs may require to be compared etc. In addition, it has been agreed that the originals of certain documents (e.g. those include service termination form if any) may require to be transferred in their original paper form. However it has been accepted by the Licensees that this can be done after the Port has taken place at intervals that may be agreed upon by the concerned parties and therefore will not affect the overall Porting time.
3.1.4 It is expected that the response time for the production of Acknowledgement Messages shall under normal circumstances be less than one minute.
3.2 Negotiation Phase
3.2.1 The PS requests the Number Porting service from the RL.
3.2.2 The RL shall be responsible to verify the identity of the PS. A PS acquisition form (in line with the RL service forms) is completed by the PS or the RL's sales agent and submitted for entry into the RL's internal provisioning process. A DL service termination form (if any, that shall be defined by the DL and approved by the TRA) for the DL is completed and signed by the PS. The RL and the DL should try to approve a standard Porting Form and recommend this to the TRA for its approval.
3.2.3 Upon completion and submission of the PS acquisition form, Application form and the DL service termination form (if any), the RL performs its initial checks to confirm that Number Portability can be applied.
3.2.4 If RL's checks are passed, the RL sends a Porting Request to the DL which must include the information listed below, and may include such additional information as is agreed by the Licensees:
(a) Unique Porting Request Reference number;
(b) PS Name;
(c) PS ID type and number;
(d) if the Porting Request is being submitted by a corporate PS, a copy of that company's valid trade licence, in addition to a copy of the valid passport or UAE identify card, (or such other identification as is acceptable to the Licensees), of the person signing on behalf of the corporation;
(e) if the Porting Request is being submitted by an individual PS in person, a copy of the valid passport or UAE identify card (or such other identification as is acceptable to the Licensees) of the PS;
(f) if the Porting Request is being submitted by an individual PS through the use of a representative, a valid power of attorney appointing that representative to act on behalf of the PS, in addition to a copy of the valid passport or UAE identify card, (or such other identification as is acceptable to the Licensees) of the representative and of the PS;
(g) signed service termination form (if any and original could be sent to the DL);
(h) PS billing address; (if applicable);
(i) Number(s) to be Ported;
(j) the identity of the DL;
(k) the identity of the RL; and
(l) And any additional relevant information (which is to be included in a "free form text field"),
3.3 Information to be provided to the PS at the time of Porting Request The following information shall be provided by the RL to the customers when they are requesting to Port Number(s):
3.3.1 a Pre-Paid PS should be advised that any existing credit may be lost (i.e. use it or lose it);
3.3.2 a Post-Paid PS should be advised that refund of any existing account credit is carried out in line with the PS contract with DL and/or refund policies of the DL;
3.3.3 the PS should be advised about limitation of services that could be applied:
(a) Partial service limitation during the period from activation of new SIM card by RL until Port activation by DL (i.e. from NP RFS message till NP Activate message, see Annex 2). Active calls of the PS during this period may be prematurely cleared down;
(b) Possibility for a pre-paid PS, to be service limited by DL for international roaming services if DL is not able to apply instant billing / deduction from the subscriber's credit.
(c) Possibility for limitation on Post-Paid PS by the DL from accessing shared-revenue services by DL.
3.3.4 The PS must be informed that the RL is requesting the DL to terminate the service currently being provided to the PS related to the Porting Number, but that the post-paid PS remains liable for any outstanding debts owed to the DL.
3.3.5 The details of the procedures and timescale for undertaking the Porting Request.
3.3.6 The conditions for Porting back and Cancellation of Porting Request.
3.3.7 That a secondary Number can only be Ported at the same time as the corresponding primary Number. If a secondary Number is not Ported at the same time as the corresponding primary Number, then any services provided on the secondary Number may be lost.
3.4 Timeline for the exchange of information once the Porting Request has been completed
3.4.1 The time requirements for the DL to respond to the various messages are shown in Annex 2.
3.4.2 All Porting Requests should be submitted by the RL to the DL on Normal Working Days. Porting Requests received on non-Working Days will be treated as though they had been received on the first Working Day.
3.4.3 The processes between the initial Porting Request and Port activation time shall be done within one Working Day. For example: if the initial Porting Request was received by DL at 1:00PM on a Working Day, this Port activation shall happen before 1:00PM on the next Working Day.
3.4.4 On receipt of the Porting Request from the RL, the DL performs checks to confirm that the Number can be Ported. The only circumstances in which a Porting Request can be rejected by the DL are listed in Annex 5.
3.4.5 If the checks are passed, then the DL sends to the RL a "Port Accept Message",
3.4.6 If the RL receives the "Port Accept Message" then performs any necessary verification and then issues a NP RFS message. The RL should have activated the PS service before sending this NP RFS message, the DL shall carry out the necessary steps to activate the Porting of the Number(s) concerned and then send a message to the National Porting System confirming the successful activation (NP Activate message).
3.4.7 If the Porting Request fails any of the criteria given Annex 5 then the DL will send a Porting Request Reject message to the RL. This Porting Request Rejection will contain all the reason(s) for rejection (as defined in Annex 5).
3.4.8 On receipt of the Porting Request Reject message, depending on the nature of the problem(s), the RL can issue a revised Porting Request to the DL. A revised Porting Request must comply with all the requirements of a new Porting Request.
3.5 Cancellation of the Porting Request
An Application can be cancelled by the PS or RL at any time before the send- ing of Porting Request message. It will not be possible to cancel a Porting Request after sending the Porting Request to the DL, however the PS can request to Port back within 3 (three) Working Days from a successful Port ac- tivation.
3.6 Provisioning Phase
During the provisioning phase the DL and the RL undertake the activities necessary to prepare to deactivate and activate the PS service. These provi- sioning activities should not interfere with the existing routing of calls to and from the mobile PS who is still using the services provided by DL.
3.7 Completion Phase
For MNP Porting (except for pre-order Porting Request), the Porting Process should be completed within this phase.
3.7.1 The Porting sequence shall always be: activation of the account with the RL by RL followed by deactivation of the account with the DL by DL
3.7.2 Where the PS has not requested a specific date and time for the Porting, Porting shall be activated as soon as possible after the 'Port Accept" message has been sent from the DL to the RL.
3.7.3 Within one hour from receiving "Port Accept" message, the RL will send Activation SMS to the PS, and will activate the service and send Number Porting ready-for-service "NP RFS" message to the National Porting System.
3.7.4 After the RL sends "NP RFS" message to the National Porting System, Porting will be activated within the National Porting System, and copies of the changes will be broadcasted to the Licensees.
3.7.5 After receiving "NP RFS Broadcast" message, the DL will send disconnection SMS to the PS, then disconnect the service on DL's SIM card and carry out the necessary steps to activate the Porting of the Number(s) concerned, and then send "NP Activate" message to the National Porting System confirming the successful activation.
3.7.6 If the PS is on active call at the time of the DL disconnecting the relevant service, the DL may (at DL's discretion) force-release the existing connection of the PS, without waiting for it to be idle, in order to carry out the Porting, it is to be noted that emergency calls should not be force-released.
3.7.7 The RL is responsible for the successful completion of the Porting. If, for any reason, the Porting is unsuccessful, the case will be treated as a fault. The RL will be responsible for managing the fault through to resolution, and the DL has an obligation to work closely with the RL to solve any problem.
3.7.8 In exceptional circumstances it may be necessary to terminate the process and for the PS's service to be returned, temporarily, to the DL. This process Termination' will be performed only to prevent a serious interruption in service. Once the PS is restored to service with the DL, the RL will resolve the issue which prevented the Port activation from happening and will then represent the Port as a new Porting Request which will carry the normal time-scales as detailed above.
3.7.9 The RL will send an SMS to the PS upon successful completion of the Porting activation.
3.8 Implications of the Completion Phase for the customer
The following implications may arise for the PS during the completion phase (more specifically the duration between receiving "Port Accept Message" and sending "NP Activate" message)
3.8.1 Any call (except emergency calls) be made by the PS during the Completion Phase up to the disconnection of the service by the DL may be force-released.
3.8.2 The service of the RL may be activated at any time during the Completion Phase,
3.8.3 Although there may be an indication that the service of the RL has already been activated, the connectivity of the PS (both for inbound and outbound calls) may be uncertain during the Porting Window (as various databases are being updated).
4. Relinquishing Ported Numbers:
4.1 A Ported Number shall be regarded as relinquished when the PS's service has been terminated with the RL for 6 (six) months period (quarantine period).
4.2 If a Ported Number is relinquished, the Ported Number should be returned to the Number Range Holder (see Annex 4).
5. Dispute Resolution
Any disputes arising relating to the provisions or application of these Porting Process Specifications, may be referred by either of the Licensees or the MNP provider to the Management Committee for review and recommendations.
6. Fault Handling
Each Licensee is responsible for its own customer base and as such has control of any service fault reports. For Ported Number Numbers, fault handling for the Ported Number is under the control of the RL.

MNP or Mobile Number Portability The capability or service whereby a subscriber who is being provided with a Telecommunications Service for mobile communications by the DL in association or connection with a particular Number, can terminate the contract with the DL for provision of that Telecommunications Service, but continue to use that Number in association or connection with the provision of a Telecommunications Service by the RL. The term to "Port" shall mean with respect to a specific Number, the retention by the relevant subscriber of the use of that Number while changing the identity of the provider of the Telecommunications Service associated with the use of that Number, and the terms "Porting", "Ports", "Ported" and "Porting Process" shall all have corresponding meanings.
Number a number associated with a subscriber for the use of a Telecommunications Service, where that telephone number is in accordance with the National Numbering Plan, of the format (05X XXXXXXX).
Working Day a day consisting of a period of 24 hours ending at midnight excluding weekends (Friday and Saturday) and public holidays.
Working Hour An hour within the period from 8am to 8pm on a Working Day
National Porting System or NPS A common centralized system used for mobile Number Porting that comprises a centralized database using an ACQ solution and an Order Handling Platform.
Numbering Range Holder The Licensee to whom the Number block containing the Ported Number has been allocated by the TRA
Order Handling Platform A centralized platform that supports Number Portability processes among Licensees for responding to and initiating subscriber Port Requests including without limitation the exchange of messages among participating Licensees, validation of these messages, and the notification of executed Ports to all Licensees.

Annex 5 – Reasons for rejection of the Porting Request by the DL
The Porting Request can only be rejected by the DL for any of the reasons given below,
The message in brackets is used as the reason for rejection.
The Number is not allocated to the DL, or any resellers, partners or subsidiaries of the DL. ("Number not Allocated")
The Number is not held by a PS of the DL, or any resellers of the DL. ("Not Assigned")
A Porting Request is associated with inactive Number ("Inactive Number")
The identification document(s) received by the DL is invalid (in line with the requirements set-out in this document) ("Invalid Identification")
The Number, PS Name do not match the data held by the DL ("Identification Mismatch"), however the DL shall recognize that names may have alternative spellings which should be taken into consideration.
A previous report of stolen / lost handset / SIM card by the customer ("Lost / Stolen")
Double Porting ("Double Porting")
Second Porting Request within one month from previous successful Port excluding Port-back within 3 Working Days ("Porting Request Limit exceeded")
Incomplete Porting Request ("Incomplete Porting Request") as per criteria set in section 3.2 (d).
When a post-paid PS is subject to suspension of incoming and/or outgoing calls at the time of Porting Request due to unpaid bills as set-out in this document ("Under Suspension")
The Number is a secondary Number that is requested to be Ported without Porting of the primary Number in the same request.

Annex 7 – Licensee's Daily MNP capacity
a. The daily capacity shall be initially counted as:
("centralized database yearly capacity" divided by 250) multiplied by 150% which represents the Daily Capacity (Ports per working-day by Licensees)
b. The Daily Capacity shall initially be equally divided between the Licensees which represents the Initial Daily Capacity per Licensee.
c. If the Daily Capacity per Licensee is exceeded, then it is expected to have more delays in processing Applications, however the TRA encourages the Licensees to work on a fully automated process that allow more capacity and lower (or no) human intervention.
d. RL shall try to queue Porting Requests above RL's agreed Daily Capacity
e. Measuring the amount of "queued" Porting Requests on a daily basis, shall be the responsibility of the RL and National Porting System.
f. The Licensees shall meet (if required) every second week (on Mondays) during the first three months after the MNP implementation, to evaluate the actual utilisation of the Daily Capacity of the last period, based on official Porting information from the National Porting System, Porting information from Licensees, as well as information on measurements of queued Porting Requests, provided by the National Porting System or by the Licensees. Meeting frequency thereafter, can be relaxed as agreed upon by Licensees and the TRA.
g. Based on the actual utilisation together with current market needs, the Licensees will, during these meetings if needed, approve and recommend changes of one or more of the following parameters;
a. Yearly capacity of the centralized database (to be implemented in accordance with the terms and conditions of the contract with the National Porting System)
b. Daily Capacity (implementation time to be agreed by Licensees and approved by the TRA)
