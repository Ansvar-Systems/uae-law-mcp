Proposal for the Program of: Master of Science in Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems October, 2003
Summary of the Proposed Master of Science in Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems
Admission Requirements
- B.Sc. degree with an overall Grade Point Average (GPA) of 2.5 (C+) or better in the Field of applied sciences and social sciences. Experienced users in the Field of remote sensing and GIS are encouraged to apply even their GPA is less than 2,5. A limited number of students will be accepted based on preferences.
- Proficiency in English is required according to the Graduate Studies guidelines, which requires a score of 550 in TOEFL, or IELTS score of 6, or 70% or higher in the University English Examination administered by the University General Requirements Unit (UGRU). Student scoring TOEFL between 450 and 550 would be required to take 1-2 courses of technical English. Those who have studied their B.Sc. in an English-based country can be exempted from the English requirements.
- Passing an interview.
- Employed candidates are required to submit a written permission to pursue graduate studies from their employers.
Participating Colleges
The program gets its unique strength because of its interdisciplinary nature. The courses included in the program will be taught by Faculty members from the colleges of Humanities and Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Information Technology, and Business and Administration.
Language: English will be used as a medium of instruction for the program.
Modes of Enrollment: Students may enroll as full- time or as part-time in a thesis or non-thesis track.
Duration: 2 years for full-time and 3 to 4 years for part-time.
Expected number of students: 10 per year.
Degree Requirements
The M.Sc. in Remote sensing and GIS program consists of two options:
Option I (with M.Sc. thesis): Student must complete 30 credit hours for graduation. This is divided among core courses (18 credit hours), elective courses (6 credit hours), and M.Sc. thesis (6 credit hours).
Option 2 (Non-thesis): Student must complete 34 credit hours for graduation. This is divided among core courses (18 credit hours), elective courses (12 credit hours), and a capstone research project (4 credit hours).
In both options the majority of courses will be offered in evening, so working engineers and other professionals can attend.
Degree Awarded
Having successfully completed the taught component, the award of the M.Sc. is based on satisfactory performance in the courses and the thesis or research project. Students must achieve at least an overall GPA of 3.0 to graduate.

PART I: Definition of the Program
I.I INTRODUCTION
The importance of the spatial dimension in examining issues such as regional development, land use planning and environmental monitoring is widely recognized. In work of this kind, it is necessary to interrelate information derived from sources such as censuses, maps, surveys, aerial photographs and satellite imagery, each of which may utilize a variety of methods of spatial referencing. The methods required for these types of investigation and the analysis of the resulting databases, which link information to maps stored in digital form, constitute the field of Remote Sensing and Geographical Information Systems (GIS).
Application of GIS in the UAE is of high demand in municipalities, telecommunication, water, electricity, oil, and coastal sectors. A survey conducted by one of the staff from the UAE University has shown that the need for GIS professionals in the UAE is between 1 to 15 persons per department (Appendix B).
The UAE University is well recognized as one of the leading centers in the area in the field of Geographical Information Systems. GIS and related technologies (including remote sensing, photogrammetry, survey data acquisition and digital mapping) are taught at faculties of Engineering, Humanities and Social Sciences, and Sciences since 1990s.
The proposed program gets its unique strength because of its interdisciplinary nature. The courses included in the program will be taught by staff from the faculties of Humanities and Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, and College of Information Technology. Available facilities at these faculties (Faculty members, staff, computing, data, references, journals) provide the foundation for practical and research applications. Members of staff maintain close links, in terms of research projects and consultancy, with a number of organizations which make extensive use of spatially referenced data. A wide variety of software for Remote Sensing and GIS applications is available, with close relationships being maintained with vendors.
1.2 RELEVANCE TO THE U.A.E.
In the UAE, many organizations continue to experience rapid growth in the volumes of spatial data. Unfortunately, severe shortages of skilled personnel, combined with a lack of understanding of the potential uses of GIS methods, mean that these same organizations have continuing difficulties in managing these complex problems. Since the late 1980s interest in the field of GIS has been burgeoning, and the pace of new developments continues to grow.
The proposed M.Sc. in Remote Sensing and GIS at the UAE University will be the first educational programs of its kind in the area. The M.Sc. is designed to provide a unique educational opportunity in this subject area, and improve the understanding of the field of Remote Sensing and GIS, together with its implications and societal ratifications
The program is expected to attract applicants from a variety of backgrounds, including recent graduates in the social, earth and environmental sciences, engineering, together with managers, researchers and technical specialists from commerce, industry and government organizations. As part of the educational process, the program aims to bring together a diversity of individuals into each course group, with their varied range of experiences. Course participants are expected to come from the UAE, Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC), the Middle East, and other countries.
1.3 OBJECTIVES OF THE PROGRAM
- To provide an intensive educational opportunity designed to equip students for either a career in the Remote Sensing and Geographical Information industry or for further research.
- To develop detailed knowledge and understanding of recent advances in Remote Sensing, GIS, computing technology, and software engineering methods that are relevant to Remote Sensing and GIS.
- To build up a high level of practical skills and experience in the use of Remote Sensing and Geographical Information System tools.
1.4 OTHER EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES OF THE PROGRAM
- To achieved the program's vision, goals, and objectives, the following additional educational objectives are planned:
- To provide students with opportunities throughout the curriculum to develop computational techniques, and written and oral communication skills.
- To provide students with the recognition and understanding of the professional and societal responsibilities associated with working in the industry, environment, and administration.
- To provide students and staff with an environment in which they can address global and regional environmental problems and investigate models of solution, sharpen their aesthetic sense, and embrace their own social and personal development.

The program will be managed and coordinated by the Dean of Graduate Studies and an executive committee. The departments in the UAE that involved in Remote Sensing and GIS are diverse (refer to Appendix B). The interdisciplinary nature of the program requires candidates to work in association with and attend courses given by a number of departments in the University, Major contribution are planned from the following Colleges:
- Humanities and Social Sciences, Geography Department.

- Information Technology.

Available facilities at these colleges (staff, computing, data, references, journals) provide the foundation for practical and research applications. A wide variety of software for Remote Sensing and GIS applications is available in the UAE University (see PART 3), with close relationships being maintained with vendors such as the ER MAPPER, ERDAS, Environmental Systems Research Institute (ESRI), AutoDesk, and Oracle.
1.6 CAPACITY OF THE PROGRAM
Based on the currently available human resources in the Geography, Geology, Civil engineering and Information Technology departments that may participate .in the program (about 19 faculty members) and material facilities (fully equipped laboratories), the coordinated program is capable of recruiting and graduating approximately 10 highly qualified students each year, leading to a long –term graduate student body of 50-60 students.

2.3 ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS
Students seeking admission to the program must submit an official application form. That application form has to be accompanied by an official transcript from the university from which the applicant received his/her Bachelor Degree. A complete application documents should be received by the Deanship of Graduate Studies before the announced deadline. The following admission rules apply:
* Applicants should be holding a B.Sc. degree in Science or Applied Sciences form the UAE University or another accredited university. Students from political science, sociology, history, and other similar fields can apply to the program and may require to take background courses to upgrade their skills.
* Applicants should have gained a minimum cumulative Grade Point Average (GPA) of 2.5 (grade of C+). Those with grade less than C+, but with good experience will be given a chance to prove their ability.
* Proficiency in English is required according to the Graduate Studies guidelines, which requires a score of 550 in TOEFL, or IELTS score of 6, or 70% or higher in the University English Examination administered by the University General Requirements Unit (UGRU). Student scoring TOEFL between 450 and 550 would be required to take 1-2 courses of technical English. Those who have studied their B.Sc. in an English-based country can be exempted from the English requirements.
* Applicants must complete a personal interview conducted by the Graduate Studies committee of the University. Admission is decided on a competitive basis.
* If employed, applicants should receive permission from their place of employment for enrolment in the program.
* Applicant may be required to pass remedial undergraduate courses but the grades in these courses are not included in the GPA of the graduate courses.
2.4 ACADEMIC ADVISING GUIDELINES
Students are advised at two stages during the program study as follows:
* During their first year in the program, students have to be advised by a committee of two advisors nominated by the Program Executive Committee (one for female students and the other for the male students).
* As the students choose their thesis/capstone research project topic, their thesis advisor(s) becomes their academic advisor.
* Advisors are responsible for informing students about relevant rules and regulations, especially regulations pertinent to academic probation. Advisors help students developing their study plans as students choose their thesis/capstone research project topic. Advisors co-sign their students' registration form each semester and thereby oversee all registration, add/drop, and withdrawal actions by their students.
* Through their supervision, advisors insure that students follow the "model study plan" as close as possible, and thereby follow any prerequisites, finish the core courses as early as possible, choose proper electives, and finish their theses and all other requirements for satisfaction of the degree. Prior to nominating any student for graduation, the program advisor review student's academic records in a special form to assure that he/she has completed all requirements: The Program Executive Committee normally discusses odd cases that arise and require University approval before seeking this approval.
* Both the advisor and the program executive committee chair keep file for each student. This file contains all relevant records issued by the registrar, and the status of any credits earned prior to transfer of students, if any, between programs within University.

2.14 COURSES DESCRIPTION
Catalog description of both core and elective courses are listed below:
CORE COURSES
RGIS601: Principles of Remote Sensing (2 cr. hr.)
An introduction to the principles of Remote Sensing. This course explores the techniques by which the surface of the Earth may be remotely imaged. A practical introduction to a wide variety of remote sensing data types and an essential foundation in the basic theory of remote sensing: Electromagnetic radiation, aerial photographs, the earth resource satellites, optical sensors (e.g. Landsat, SPOT, IKONOS, QuickBird), microwave sensors (e.g. LIDAR, ESRS, JERS, RADARSAT), visual analyses and Field applications.
RGIS602: Fundamentals of Geographical Information Systems (3 cr. hr.)
This course traces the origins and development of GIS, outlining the differences between GIS and the related technologies of digital mapping, provide a clear understanding and management of common GIS database systems. The different models that GIS employs to represent real-world entities are reviewed (Earth-map relationship, map projection, coordinate systems, raster, vector). Elements of graphic design and communication are reviewed with the intention of ensuring results are comprehensible and effectively portrayed. Introduction to hardware, software, and methods of data collection is provided. A series of linked practical reinforce lecture material and provide a hands-on introduction tailored for new users of GIS.
RGIS603: Digital Image Processing in Remote Sensing (3 cr. hr.)
Advanced techniques of image processing and analysis for remotely sensed digital data. Topics include radiometric correction, geometric correction, atmospheric and ground effects, image enhancement, spectral analysis, color processing, math operation, image filtering, Hyper-spectroscopy and imaging spectroscopy, noise suppression, image classification, post-classification and change detection, practical exercises based on satellite datasets and other forms of remotely sensed data. The course develops and expands topics in the area of image processing as a necessary pre-requisites to advanced studies in remote sensing. Hands-on and lab exercises complement the course.
RGIS604: Spatial Analysis Using GIS (3 cr. hr.)
This course explores methods of analyzing spatial data in the interactive and graphical environment of a GIS. The course draws on related theory in spatial statistics, geo-statistics, geographical analysis and cartographic modeling to provide a set of generic techniques for GIS users. Topics include the analysis of point patterns, networks, overlay analysis, spatial interaction models, and visualization of spatial data (virtual reality, simulation of landscape, animation, multi-media). The course concludes by considering how to extend the spatial analytical capabilities of GIS and points to the evolution of spatial decision support systems. Associated exercises and hands-on allow methods to be applied in a GIS context.
RGIS605: Location Based Services and Web-based GIS (2 cr. hr.)
The course focuses on the new techniques for linking between telecommunication (mobile equipments). Global Positioning Systems (GPS), and GIS (Location Based Services-LBS), and deployment of spatial data on the Internet (Web-based- GIS).
The course is designed to be offered jointly by the Department of Architectural Engineering and Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering with input from the College of IT and the Department of Geography,
RGIS606: Database Management Systems (2 cr. hr.)
The course provides a background about Data Base Management System (DBMS) as an integral and essential part in GIS. Topics covered include data models and sub languages for relational, hierarchical, and network approaches to database management systems. Normal forms. External models, views, implementation, data independence, concurrency control, integrity control. Project.
RGIS607: Seminar on management issues in remote sensing and GIS (I cr. hr.)
Topics cover in the seminar include presentation of students to their research topics, open GIS, management, social, legal, economical, and standardization issues related to remote sensing and GIS. The course is designed to attract participants from different colleges and the industry.
STAT659: Applied Statistics (Geo-Statistics) (2 cr. hr.)
Computer-based methods in geographical analysis. Focuses on bivariate and multivariate regression, discrimination analysis, factor analysis, log-linear models and analysis of spatial and temporal data.
ELECTIVE COURSES
RGIS610: Spatial Data Collection for GIS (2 cr. hr.)
This course introduces methods of surveying field collection of data in a manner suitable for spatial analysis. Topics will include plane and topographic surveying, use of the levels, total stations, and the Global Positioning System (GPS), preparation of data for conversion to a digital format, map generation from surveying field data, accuracy, and quality of spatial data. The course has a main field and laboratory components.
RGIS611: Advanced Remote Sensing (2 cr. hr.)
Advanced Remote Sensing is designed to provide students with knowledge of biophysical-quantitative remote sensing. Both the theoretical basis and practical aspects of this approach to remote sensing are addressed. Topics examine include remote sensing applications in natural environment such as meteorology, oceanography, hydrology, and biomass detection. This course has a higher practical component and few lectures.
RGIS612: Satellite Positioning (2 cr. hr.)
This course is directed towards the study of satellite geodesy - reference frames, coordinate transformations, time, satellite motion, and ephemeris generation. Geodetic satellites, GPS precise positioning - observation procedures, static and real-time positioning, reductions to local datum, mathematical models. Satellite Laser. Ranging, Very Long Baseline Interferometry, and satellite altimetry. Hands-on experience with GPS positioning. Case studies and applications.
RGIS613: Software Engineering (2 cr. hr.)
Methods in software system design, development, and maintenance. Formal models of structured programming and program correctness. Software engineering methods and tools. Functional and object-oriented design. Software documentation. Version tracking in large projects. Testing. Working in teams, students organize, manage, and develop a software engineering project. The course is intended to lay a foundation for those interested in GIS programming and customization (with hands-on Visual Basic and C++).
RGIS614: Selected Topics (2 cr. hr.)
Selected topics may be pursued in the forum of individually supervised projects linked to studies in postgraduate programs offered at the UAEU. This course is designed to give a student more time to understand and practice with remote sensing: and GIS related to his area of specialization. For example, developing a GIS application for utilities (water, gas, electricity), transportation (fleet management, finding the best route, monitoring of transportation network), or applying remote sensing for coastal monitoring.
RGIS615: Project Management (2 cr. hr.)
The course provides students with the skills essential for project success in today's tough GIS business environment. Acquire a streamlined, best-practices approach to planning and managing remote sensing and GIS projects of any size. The course focuses on better management of people .tools, and processes to accomplish a GIS project within time, budget, and according to specifications. The course also highlights areas of spatial data policy (institutional frames and copy .rights).
RGIS616: Transport Applications of GIS (GIS-T) (2 cr. hr.)
This course explores the spatial aspects of regional transportation systems including the description of network structure and flows. Topics include Introduction to models for analyzing multi-modal transportation flows and congestion over urban road and transit networks. These models include trip generation, trip distribution, modal split, and trip assignment and routing algorithms. Applications are developed in a Geographical Information Systems environment. Models are extended to urban land use, spatial safety analysis, pavement management system and air quality applications.
RGIS617: Urban and Environmental Applications of GIS/Remote Sensing (2 cr. hr.)
This course focuses on the application of remote sensing and GIS techniques to solving real-world urban and environmental problems. Applications may include analyses of urban and suburban landscape, land use and land cover, and communication, vegetation and forestry, biodiversity and ecology, water and water quality control, soils and minerals, geology and geomorphology, etc.
RGIS618: Remote Sensing and GIS for Petroleum (GIS-P) (2 cr. hr.)
This course explores algorithms for spatial analysis, 3D modeling in GIS, types satellite data and GIS data used for conducting optimum oil exploration operations, uses of GIS in mineral and hydrocarbon exploration; and in geological, and structural Studies. Advanced application of multi spectral imagery, radar and other remote sensing data to oil industry environments with emphasis on the different digital image processing techniques utilized for specific petroleum problem, volume distribution of petroleum products, thematic mapping of petrol stations by brand, ownership, price, volume, shop size or by any geography, develop sales territories, locating optimal position of a new outlet. Further develops hands-on skills with industry-standard GIS software for application in geologic and geophysical analysis.

ENVR6205:Coastal Management (2 cr. hr.)

ENVR6402: Environmental Geology (2 cr. hr.)

ENVR6507: Environmental Planning and Impact Analysis (2 cr. hr.)

The main topics covered in this course include: introduction to international environmental policy, environmental problems in cities, from town planning to environmental planning, urban ecological planning, information and decisions in environmental planning, environmental impact assessment (EIA), methods of public participation, and social assessment, planning for sustainable cities: Environmental management and Planning (EPM).
ENVR6510: Urban and Regional Planning (2 cr. hr.)
The course covers aspects of rational planning: comprehensive theories, planning theories, incremental, strategic and mixed scanning. Methods for setting planning objectives, development and evaluation of alternative plans, implementation, and decision-making processes are also covered. Finally, this course examines current regional planning programs, with regard to both their development and implementation in the context of various institutional structures, arrangements and intergovernmental relations.
WATR602: Water Resources Management (2 cr. hr.)
Water resources, demands, supplies, planning, and development. Reuse and disposal of reused water. Economic valuation techniques and impact analysis. Human resources development. Social aspects of water resources development. Institutional, environmental, and legal aspects.

Part IV : Comparison with International Programs
Graduate programs on Remote Sensing and Geographic Information systems are being offered by tremendous number of universities and research centers all over the world. The UAEU program of courses for Remote Sensing and Geographic Information systems compares well with parallel programs in several high quality universities, among which are the Universities of Purdue, Boston, New South Wales, Texas, Pittsburgh and the Institute for Space Studies of Catalonia (see Table 4.1 and 4.2). The courses most popularly offered at all of these universities, and also represented at UAEU, are Principles of Remote Sensing, Fundamental of GIS, Digital Image Processing, Spatial Analysis Using GIS, Spatial Data Collection for GIS, and Database Management Systems.
The UAEU program highlights the inclusion of topics such as Coastal Management, Urban and Regional Planning, and Water Resources Management. The Coastal Management topic is an important issue in the Gulf area in both studying the coast environment and exercising good management of coastal area, as we note that population are concentrated on coastal cities. Urban and Regional Planning evokes urbanization, ecological and social consequences in cities, a main application area needed in the modern UAE. Equally important is the issue of spatial agglomerations on regional growth. These issues are of high importance in areas such as the Middle East, where we are facing the problem of population migration toward the urban centers. The Water Resources Management is very important topic in the Gulf area, which has a very limited water resource, and it needs a good management supported by GIS to maintain a sustainable development of the area. These issues are direct application of Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems.
Selected topics in Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems provide the program with flexibility in order to enhance and complement the student's knowledge.
