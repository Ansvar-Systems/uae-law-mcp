Ministerial Decree 567 for 2014
Revision 3.0, August 2014
REGULATIONS FOR ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL IN THE QUARRYING INDUSTRY
Article 1 – Overview
The Ministry of Environment & Water is initiating all efforts for the protection of the environment and for sustainable growth of the quarrying industry, and to preserve and conserve the natural resources in the United Arab Emirates.
Further to the ministerial decree (492) of 2008, and (110) of 2010, amendments have been made in this revised ministerial order with additional articles. This will ensure best management & operational practices, and installation of the best available techniques to minimize environmental, health and safety issues.
Additional details have been added with respect to environmental management system, ecology and quarry rehabilitation.
Article 2 – Scope of Application
2.1 The implementation of these regulations is applicable for all the new and existing quarries and crushers.
2.2 These regulations shall also apply to all the existing quarries and crushers of cement plants, ceramic and terrazzo title factories, marble factories and other related industries.
2.3 These regulations shall also apply to all the construction projects involving rock excavations, quarrying and crushing operations.
Article 3 – Regulation for Quarrying Operations
All quarrying operations in the UAE must comply with the following regulations for their day to day quarry operations with respect to drilling, blasting material handling, in-site hauling and transportation.
3.1 Drilling
3.1.1 All drilling equipment must be fitted with an efficient dust collection system.
Proper disposal arrangements must be made for the fine dust collected in bags or filters.
3.1.2 All the drilling operators and supervising staff must adopt proper safety measures during drilling operations, including Personal Protection Equipment.
3.1.3 Hours operation: Drilling operations can be carried out round the clock. If the quarry is located within 1 km from any local residential areas, the hours of operation must be restricted by the local competent authorities.
3.1.4 All the drilling and ancillary equipment equipped must be maintained in good working order to reduce fumes and noise.
3.1.5 For large drilling equipment with cab for operators, the cabs must have good sealing and fitted with dust filters and air conditioning.
3.1.6 Secondary jackhammer drilling and blasting is prohibited to reduce the size of large boulders. Hydraulic breakers must be used for reducing the size of large rock boulders.
3.2 Quarry Blasting
3.2.1 All rock blasting operations must be carried out by setting the optimum blasting parameters to reduce dust emissions, ground vibrations, air overpressure, fly rock and noise. Records of all the blasting parameters shall be maintained for better control and optimization. The parameters include:
- Proper spacing of blast holes depending on hole diameter.
- Maintaining a safe drilling angle for the quarry face.
- Calculating optimum quantity of explosives for charging.
- Use of delay detonators for reducing ground vibrations.
- Maintaining proper stemming depth to minimize fly rock.
- Avoid using detonator cords over the ground.
- Maintain proper log of all the parameters.
The above parameters must be set the blasting/explosives expert in charge of the quarry.
3.2.2 All blasting operations must be supervised and carried out by competent personnel only, as per the guidelines/rules set by the Ministry of Interior.
3.2.3 Blasting should not be carried out in adverse weather conditions. Avoid blasting when the wind direction is toward local residential areas.
3.2.4 Progressive warning signals by siren and red flags must be given prior to final detonation, to alert all the staff and public to keep more than 500 meters away from the blast area.
3.2.5 Ground vibrations and air over pressure must be monitored regularly during the blasting operations. The limits are given under Article (11). The measurements should be taken from a distance of 800 meters from the blasting face towards the nearest building or residential area. If the building or residential area is located less than 800 meters from the blasting face, then the measurements must be taken close to the foundation of the nearest building.
3.2.6 A report showing the total explosives consumption and average powder factor (tons or rock/kg. of explosive) along with the vibration and noise records must be submitted every month to the Ministry of Environment & Water.
3.3 Material Handling
Material handling shall include all the quarry operations related to cleaning of quarry face with bull dozers or excavators, loading of material by excavators/shovels to dumpers or trucks, use of hydraulic breakers for breaking large size rocks, etc.
3.3.1 Regular mechanical maintenance must be carried out on all the material handling equipment to cut down fumes and noise. Safe practices must be adopted at all times during the operations. Only licensed and trained operators shall operate the equipment.
3.3.2 All the equipment operator cabs must be properly sealed for dust and noise and fitted with air conditioning and dust filters.
3.3.3 All the grizzlies and rotary screens in the quarry area for grading the quarry run material shall be fully encapsulated with an efficient dust control system (Ref Article 7).
3.3.4 Dozing of quarry blasted material from higher levels (more than 20 meters from ground level) to lower levels is prohibited. Exceptions can be made during the initial quarry development stages only, for a maximum period of 3 months.
3.4 Hauling in Site
3.4.1 Only approved heavy duty dump trucks must be utilized for transporting quarry run material to the crushing plant.
3.4.2 Hauling of quarry material to the crushing plant by dump trucks can be carried out round the clock. In case of disturbance to the local residents, the operating hours must be restricted by the local competent authorities.
3.4.3 Hauling roads must be planned with low gradients (<10%) and set to follow safe speed limits with proper caution boards.
3.4.4 Hauling roads must be paved with asphalt or sprayed with water to prevent fugitive dust.
3.4.5 Proper caution boards showing speed limits, road conditions and directions must be placed.
3.5 Quarry Planning
3.5.1 The quarrying operations must be planned and designed with proper benching. The quarry face height must not exceed the height of the excavator bucket’s operating height and be limited to 20 meters.
3.5.2 Quarry operations in valleys and flat areas and below ground level is prohibited.
3.5.3 Irregular quarry operations without properly planned benching are prohibited. This includes quarrying and dozing of loose rock at the foothills. In the mountain areas, the quarry operations must be planned with proper benching from the upper levels.
3.6 Quarry Rehabilitation/Restoration
To maintain the ecology of the quarry areas, it is important to carry out rehabilitation of the closed quarries & quarry faces, and restore the area. The quarry faces must be restored to safe natural slopes by filling with quarry run waste or by carrying out few blasts and covering the blasted material with soil or quarry run waste.
3.6.1 the establishment owner/establishment shall carry out the quarry rehabilitation as follows:
Progressive rehabilitation must be carried out to rehabilitate parts of the quarry one after another, within a reasonable period of time; not exceeding 12 months from the date of quarry is finished in this area. With good planning, the extraction of rock proceeds in a logical sequence so that depleted areas can be rehabilitated while extraction continues in other areas of the quarry. Progressive rehabilitation is beneficial in many ways as it:
- Reduces the open areas within a pit or quarry.
- Reduces soil erosion potential
- Reduces double-handling of soil/waste materials.
The site plans for quarry operations must outline the specific requirements for rehabilitation. The site plan will be the primary means of regulating rehabilitation and ensuring that the disturbance of the site is kept to a minimum. Establishment owner/establishment is legally bound to operate and rehabilitate the site in accordance with the site plan.
If the establishment owner has not performed adequate progressive or final rehabilitation in accordance with the site plan, the local authority shall issue a Rehabilitation Order requiring the establishment owner to perform the necessary rehabilitation work within a specified time period, not exceeding 12 months.
Final rehabilitation is the rehabilitation that is performed after the closure of all quarry operations or excavations of the rock and the progressive rehabilitation have been completed. As set out in the site plan, during final rehabilitation all equipment, stockpiles and buildings must be removed, and additional vegetation, such as trees must be planted on the areas that have already been progressively rehabilitated. Final rehabilitation must be completed within a period of 18 months. The competent authority in this regard should issue a letter.
3.6.2 the responsibility of quarry rehabilitation shall fall upon the establishment owner/establishment. The establishment owner must ensure that proper rehabilitation and retoration is carried out for all the quarry faces before surrendering the leased area to the local competent authority. The local competent authority shall include quarry rehabilitation and restoration with the above points as a mandatory requirement in their lease agreement with establishment owner.
Article 4 – Regulations for Crushing Plant Operations
Rock crusher plant operations include primary and secondary crushing screening, material handling and transport.
4.1 Primary Crushers
4.1.1 The primary feeder & crusher must be installed with an approved industrial structure and fully encapsulated to prevent dust and noise. The material specifications for the structure and encapsulation must be as per UAE standards and approved by the local public works department.
4.1.2 The primary feeder and crusher must be installed with an efficient dust control system (Ref. 7.5) to prevent and control dust emission during the operations to the recommended emission standards.
4.1.3 The primary feeder encapsulation must be covered with hanging half the hieght rubber belts/screen at the inlet to prevent dust emission during unloading of quarry run material by dump trucks.
4.1.4 The crushing plant control rooms must be properly sealed with air conditioning and dust filters.
4.1.5 Regular maintenance of the primary crushing plant and the dust control system must be carried out to prevent excessive dust & noise and other mechanical problems.

4.2 Secondary Crushers
4.2.1 All secondary crushers must be installed with an approved industrial structure and fully encapsulated to prevent dust and noise. The material specifications for the structure and encapsulation must be as per UAE standards and approved by the local public works department.
4.2.2 If the secondary crushers are fitted with the original manufacturers coverings to prevent dust leakage with ducts for dry dust collection system with bag filters, then a separate encapsulation is not required.
4.2.3 The secondary crushers must be installed with an efficient dust control system (Ref. 7.5) to bring down the dust emissions within the recommended emission standards.
4.2.4 The material inlet and outlet locations of the secondary crushers must be properly sealed by using rubber curtains to prevent dust leakage.
4.2.5 The secondary crushers must be regularly maintained to minimize dust & noise levels, and other mechanical problems.
4.3. Screening Plants
4.3.1 All the vibratory screening plants must be installed with an approved industrial structure and fully encapsulated to prevent dust and noise. The material specifications for the structure and encapsulation must be as per UAE standards and approved the local public works department.
4.3.2 The screening plants must be installed with an efficient dust control system (Ref. 7.5) to bring down the dust emission within the recommended standards.
4.3.3 If the screening plant is covered and sealed for dust leakage with the original manufacturers design with ducts for dry dust collection to bag filters, then a separate encapsulation is not required.
4.3.4 The material inlet and outlet chutes of the screening plants to be protected properly with rubber curtains to prevent dust leakage.
4.3.5 The screening plants must be regularly maintained to optimize the performance and minimize the dust and noise.
4.4 Material Handling
Material handling operations will include stackers, conveyor belts and ancillary units, stock piling of quarry products, in-site loading and hauling...etc.
4.4.1 All conveyor belts and stacker systems should be properly enclosed on the sides and top and installed with an efficient dust control system (Ref. 7.5).
4.4.2 For stock piling of graded material from the screening plant, the conveyors output points should have proper flexible chutes to prevent wind whipping of fine material.
4.4.3 All stock piles of graded fine material to be sprayed with water or protected with coverings to prevent fugitive dust due to wind whipping.
4.4.4 The material loading equipment (shovels/wheel loaders) must have proper sealed operator cabs with air conditioning and dust filters.
4.4.5 All the material handling equipment shall be properly maintained to minimize fumes, noise and dust emissions.
4.4.6 There must be sufficient space between the stock piles of quarry products for safe handling hauling, visibility and movement of vehicles. The minimum gap between the stockpiles must be more than 15 meters all around. Proper safety and warning signs must be placed.
4.5 Mobile Crushers
4.5.1 Mobile crushing plants must be used mainly for construction project operations.Use of mobile crushers for regular quarry operations must be prohibited.
4.5.2 The mobile crushers deployed must have original manufactures sealed coverings to minimize noise, fumes and dust.
4.5.3 The mobile crushers must have efficient dust control system to prevent dust emission during operation.
4.5.4 The mobile crusher equipment must be regular maintained to minimize emissions.
Article 5 – Transport of Quarry Products
The establishment owner/establishment must inform the truck operators of the following regulations with respect to transportation of material from the quarry and crushing plant:
5.1 All trucks transporting quarry and crusher products must be regularly maintained & inspected to ensure that they are in good running condition.
5.2 All trucks carrying crusher products to customer sites must cover the material to prevent wind whipping. Also it is recommended to spray water on top of the material in the truck. The establishment owner shall arrange to provide this facility.
5.3 The quarry run material and crushed quarry products loaded into the trucks must be below the body level of the truck/trailer. Overloading of the trucks above their carrying capacity is prohibited.
5.4 Armor rocks loaded into flatbed trailers must be properly inspected for loose joints and cracks in the rock boulders to prevent falling of the rock on to the roads. The rocks must be secured to prevent sliding and falling of the flat bed trailers.
Article 6 – Quarry and crusher Mangement
All the quarry and crusher operations must be carried out with a planned management chart, indicating the individual responsibilities. This will also include HSE management. All records must be maintained at the site.
6.1 Competent Staff
During the quarry and crusher operations, a competent and qualified quarry or mining engineer or manager must be present at the site or contactable at all times. The engineer or manager shall have the authority to provide all the information regarding quarry and crusher operations to the local competent authorities and to the auditors of the Ministry of Environment & Water.
The site foreman/supervisor must also have relevant qualifications and experience in supervising all the quarry operations.
6.2 HSE Management System
A well-planned HSE (Health, Safety & Environment) management system is a valuable tool for managerial and supervisory staff in quarries and crushers to meet current and future environmental requirements and challenges. All quarries must adopt a proper HSE management system by hiring a professional HSE consultant.
6.3 Production Reports
All establishment owner/establishment must submit their quarterly production data & reports to the local competent authority and to the Ministry of Environment & Water. The Monthly production data shall include all the types of products (armor rock, graded rocks of different sizes, aggregates, sand road base, etc.). The report must also include the consumption of fuel, electricity, water and explosives. All the records & reports shall be stored at the quarry office for a minimum period of 5 years.
6.4 Quarry plans
All establishment owner/establishment must submit annually, detailed maps drawings of the quarry and crusher to the local competent authorities and to the Ministry of Environment & Water. The plans shall include:
- Locations of the existing and abandoned quarry faces.
- Location of crushing plants.
- 3D contour maps.
- Location for ancillary or additional developments.
- Location of closed quarry faces & rehabilitated areas.
- Material storage yards.
- Waste storage yards.
- In site roads and dwellings
- Residential areas around the site.
Article 7 – Environmental Control & Monitoring
7.1 Air Quality Monitoring
- All the establishment owner/establishment must install at least one stationery ambient air quality monitor for PM2.5 (<2.5 µg/M3) and PM10 (<10 µg/M3) measurements. The monitors will be networked via internet to the local competent authority and to the Ministry of Environment & water.
- The Ministry of Environment & water must approve the type of air quality monitor that will be installed. The air quality monitors must be calibrated every 3 months to ensure accuracy of the measurements. Local and federal authorities shall be authorized to inspect to monitoring equipement from time to time.
- The establishment owner/establishment must submit their monthly air quality data to the local competent authority and to the Ministry of Environment & Water.
- Location of the stations to be selected in coordination with the local competent authority and Ministry of Environment and Water.
7.2 Inspection and Auditing
Auditors from the Ministry of Environment & water and inspectors from local authorities shall visit the quarry and crusher sites on a regular basis to assess and monitor the quarry operations and the air emissions on a regular basis to ensure that the establishment owner/establishment is complying with all the regulations under this ministerial decree.
7.3 Landscape
All establishment owners/establishments must plant sufficient trees and plants in the area to improve the landscape and ecology of the area. Trees must be planted with 3 meters spacing along the entrance roads and boundaries facing towards the main road.
7.4 Noise Monitoring & Noise Barriers
- The establishment owner/establishment must carry out regular (monthly) noise monitoring in all the quarry areas and near to the closest residential area less than 1 km. from the quarry. The noise levels must be within the limits set under Article (10) of this ministerial decree.
7.5 Dust Control System for crusher plant
Best available techniques (BAT) must be adopted to control and minimize dust emissions efficiently during the crusher plant operations. The following dust control systems have proved very effective and are acceptable:
a – High pressure (>10 bar) water with compressed air dust suppression system: The water suppression system shall have sufficient heavy duty nozzles to cover the dust areas. Foaming agents can be used for further reduction in dust. High pressure water with compressed air suppression systems also saves on water consumption and more effective at higher pressures. High pressure (>40 bar) fogging systems are also very effective in suppressing quarry dust emissions. The system must be maintained at all times for high efficiency.
b- high capacity dry dust collection system with bag filters. The capacity of the bag filters and the motors will depend on the output and the quality of the feed material. The bag filters must be regularly maintained for higher efficiency. Additional bag filters at high load points are recommended as bypass filters.
c – Combination of both the above systems is very effective in case of the feed material with high percentage of clay and soil.
7.6 Waste Management
- Proper care must be taken to dispose all waste products from quarrying and crushing operations. The natural waste products can be utilized for quarry rehabilitation/restoration work at a later stage.
- The different types of wastes must be segregated at site and stocked separately.
- Quarry areas should be protected from illegal dumping of waste by third parties.
- Separate waste collection bins for recyclables & general waste must be placed for municipal solid wastes at the quarry site and in the labor camps for workers.
- Waste oil and other residues from machinery maintenance should be stored in proper containers or cemented pits to avoid seepage to ground water and surrounding soil.
- Only authorized waste disposal contractors must be hired for collection and disposal of waste, including waste oils, batteries, tires, scrap etc.
- A proper waste management record must be maintained at site.
7.7 Environment Management Plan (EMP) and Reporting
All the establishment owners/establishments must submit an EMP report to the Ministry of Environment & Water and to their concerned local authority. The main purpose of the EMP shall be for prevention of pollution in accordance with the regulations, continued improvements to minimize environmental impacts and to adopt effective management practices. The EMP report shall include:
- Environmental objectives and targets.
- Current layout plan of the quarry and crusher areas.
- Detailed plan of the quarry showing all the excavation areas and benches as under 6.4.
- Expected quantity of rock that will be excavated from each area.
- Total reserves available
- Rehabilitation and restoration plans for the quarry faces.
- Rehabilitation of quarry areas that are not being exploited or abandoned for more than 3 years.
- Impact on ground water and surface water flow.
- Plan for land protection, develop the landscape and improve the ecology.
- Plan for minimizing pollution during quarry operations.
- Energy and resource conservation.
- Installation, operation and maintenance of dust control systems.
- Environmental monitoring for air emissions, noise and blasting monitoring Waste management plan (7.6) with records.
- Training and awareness.
- Monthly productions records including stock inventory of quarry products at the end of each year (31 December).
- Expansion plans for the quarry and crusher.
- Record of all the quarry and crusher equipment.
- Fuel, electricity and water consumption records
The EMP report shall be submitted within 3 months from the date of issue of this ministerial decree. The EMP report shall be updated every year for the period ending 31 December and submitted before 31 January of the coming / following year.
7.8 Video Monitoring & network
All the establishment owners/establishments must install video monitoring and surveillance system to cover the operation of the quarry & crusher areas. Each crushing plant line must have a separate camera connected to the network. This shall ensure the establishment owner/establishment in efficiently controlling and monitoring operational issues. The establishment owner/ establishment shall monitor and record the operations at all times on a 24-hour basis. The video monitors shall be linked through an internet network for further monitoring by the local competent authorities and the Ministry of Environment and Water. All the surveillance records must be stored at site for a minimum period of two years.
Article 8 – Environmental Compliance Certification (ECC)
All the establishment owners/establishments must obtain an environmental compliance certification (ECC) from the Ministry of Environment & water. This will be mandatory before the issue and renewal of all licenses and permits issued by the local and federal competent authorities. The auditors of the Ministry of Environment & Water shall carry out necessary inspections at the quarry site before issuing the ECC. The ECC must be renewed annually.

Article 12 – Performance Rating & Administrative Sanctions
All the establishment owners/establishments not complying with these regulations shall be penalized as per the UAE cabinet resolution (20) of 2008.
12.1 Performance Rating
The Ministry of Environment & water shall regularly assess the performance of all the establishments operations to ensure that all the establishment owners /establishments comply with these regulations.
The Ministry will rate the performance of the quarries & crushers into four main categories A, B, C & D (best to poor performance) as per the ministry’s rating procedure under Annex – 1.
12.2 Administrative Sanctions
In case of environmental, health and safety violations by establishment owner/establishment, the local competent authority or the Ministry of Environment & Water shall take necessary action to shut down the quarry operations for one to three months, or permanent closure depending on the number of the violations during each year.
* First violation – 30 days closure of all the quarry and crusher operations;
* Second violation – 90 days closure of all the quarry and crusher operations;
* Third violation – Permanent closure of all the quarry and crusher operations;
The main violations that will be considered for administrative sanctions are as below:
a) Excessive dust emission from crushing plant operations;
b) Excessive dust emission during quarry operations;
c) Excessive exhaust smoke or fumes from the engines of quarry and crusher equipment;
d) Fugitive dust emission from operational roads;
e) Improper maintenance of dust control systems and crushing plant enclosures;
f) Excessive noise from quarry and crusher operations;
g) Not observing proper health and safety procedure;
h) Irregular quarry operations without benching;
i) Absence of competent supervisors at site during quarry and crusher operations;
j) All other irregularities that are not in compliance under the articles of this ministerial decision.
Article 13 – Implementation
The above regulations must complied all the establishment owners/establishments as in Article (2) of this ministerial decree, within 180 days from the date of issue of this ministerial decree.
All the establishment owners/establishments shall submit a copy of their planning and schedule of implementing these regulations within two months from the date of this issue, to the ministry of environment & water, and to the local competent authority.
Local competent authorities and the Ministry of Environment & water shall follow up the implementation of these regulations at site by carrying out regular inspections and assessment.
