مجلس إدارة الهيئة الاتحادية للرقابة النووية ("الهيئة")
بعد الاطلاع على:
- المرسوم بقانون اتحادي رقم (6) لسنة 2009 في شأن الاستعمالات السلمية للطاقة النووية ("القانون")؛
- قرار مجلس الوزراء رقم (17/1و) لسنة 2021 بتجديد تشكيل مجلس إدارة الهيئة الاتحادية للرقابة النووية؛
- قرار مجلس الوزراء رقم (2/9و) لسنة 2020 بإعتماد دليل حوكمة مجالس الإدارة في الحكومة الاتحادية؛
- أحكام المادة (67) من القانون التي نصت على صلاحية مجلس إدارة الهيئة بتشكيل "لجنة الوقاية من الإشعاعات في الدولة" وتحديد صلاحيات اللجنة ونظام عملها؛
- قرار مجلس إدارة الهيئة رقم (15) لسنة 2021 بالتعديل رقم 4 للائحة الداخلية المنظِّمة لاجتماعات مجلس إدارة الهيئة الاتحادية للرقابة النووية؛
- قرار مجلس إدارة الهيئة رقم (4) لسنة 2011 بتشكيل لجنة الوقاية من الإشعاعات في الدولة وتحديد اختصاصاتها ونظام عملها؛
- قرار مجلس إدارة الهيئة رقم (8) لسنة 2014 بتجديد مدة عمل لجنة الوقاية من الإشعاعات؛
- قرار مجلس إدارة الهيئة رقم (5) لسنة 2016 بشأن إعادة تشكيل لجنة الوقاية من الإشعاعات في الدولة وإصدار الشروط المرجعية للجنة الوقاية من الإشعاعات؛
- قرار مجلس إدارة الهيئة رقم (17) لسنة 2019 بتجديد مدة عمل لجنة الوقاية من الإشعاعات في الدولة وإعتماد شروطها المرجعية المُعدَّلة؛
وبناءً على توصية مدير عام الهيئة، وعلى موافقة مجلس إدارة الهيئة خلال اجتماعه رقم (3) لسنة 2021 المنعقد في 19 مايو 2021،

Resolution No. (17) of 2021 of the Board of Management of the Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation
Approving the Amended Terms of Reference of the Radiation Protection Committee
The Board of Management of the Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation ("FANR"):
Having considered:
- The Federal Law by Decree No. (6) of 2009, Concerning the Peaceful Uses of Nuclear Energy (the ’’Law");
- Cabinet Resolution No. (17/1 g) of 2021 on Renewal of Formation of the Board of Management of the Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation;
- Cabinet Resolution No. (2/9g) of 2020 on the Guide to Board Governance in the UAE Federal Government;
- Provisions of Article (67) of the Law envisaging the authority of the FANR's Board of Management to establish "the Radiation Protection Committee in the State" and to specify authorities and bylaws of such committee;
- FANR's Board of Management Resolution No. (15) of 2021 on Amendment No. 4 of the By-Laws Regulating the Meetings of the Board of Management of the Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation;
- FANR’s Board of Management Resolution No. (4) of 2011 Establishing the Radiation Protection Committee in the State and determining its Authorities and Work Policy;
- FANR's Board of Management Resolution No. (8) of 2014 on Renewal of the Term of the Radiation Protection Committee;
- FANR's Board of Management Resolution No. (5) of 2016 concerning Re-Formation of the Radiation Protection Committee in the State and Issuance of the Terms of Reference of the Radiation Protection Committee;
- FANR's Board of Management Resolution No. (17) of 2019 Renewing the Term of the Radiation Protection Committee in the State and Approving its Amended Terms of Reference; and
Based on the recommendation of the Director General of FANR and on FANR's Board of Management approval during its meeting No. (3) of 2021 held on 19 May 2021,
Has decided the following:
Article 1
To approve the Amended Terms of Reference of the Radiation Protection Committee in the State, as provided in Annex 1 to this Resolution.
Article 2
This Resolution shall come into force as of the date of its issuance and shall be published in the Official Gazette.
Abdulla Al Suwaidi Chairman
Issued in Abu Dhabi
Date: 7 Shawwal 1442 AH
Corresponding to: 19 May 2021
ANNEX 1:
AMENDED TERMS OF REFERENCE OF THE RADIATION PROTECTION COMMITTEE IN THE STATE
1- Introduction
1.1- Pursuant to Article 67 of the Federal Law by Decree No. 6 of 2009 Concerning the Peaceful Uses of Nuclear Energy, “[t]he Board shall establish, by a decision, a committee to be named 'the Radiation Protection Committee in the State’, the decision shall specify the authorities and bylaws of such committee”.
1.2- On 20 July 2011, the Board of Management of the Federal Authority for Nuclear Regulation (FANR) issued Resolution No. (4) of 2011 Establishing the Radiation Protection Committee in the State and Determining its Authorities and Work Policy. The Radiation Protection Committee in the State was established for a period of three (3) years. On 14 July 2014, the Board of Management of FANR issued Resolution No. (8) of 2014 for the Renewal of the Term of the Radiation Protection Committee.
1.3- The Board of Management on 16 August 2016 issued the Resolution No. (5) of 2016 Concerning Re-Formation of the Radiation Protection Committee in the State and Issuance of the Terms of Reference of the Radiation Protection Committee. By a Resolution No. (17) of 2019, the Board of Management renewed the Term of the Radiation Protection Committee for another period of three (3) years and amended the Terms of Reference.
2- Purpose of the Terms of Reference - Amendments
2.1- The purpose of the Terms of Reference (the ‘ToR’) of the Radiation Protection Committee in the State (the ‘Committee’) is to define the objectives and scope of the work and functions of the Committee; its composition, the meetings and quorum; recommendations and voting; confidentiality; reporting; and responsibilities of its members, the Chair and Secretary.
2.3- Amendments to the ToR of the Committee shall be approved by the Board of Management of FANR upon recommendation of the Chair of the Committee.
3- Scope and Functions of the Committee
3.1- The Committee shall provide advice and consultation to FANR management and to other relevant authorities including the General Secretariat of the Cabinet on matters related to radiation protection in medical, occupational and public exposure, and environmental radiological protection, emergency preparedness and response, education and training, research and development to enhance the radiation protection in the State.
3.2- Within its scope, the Committee shall carry out the following functions:
a) work with the competent entities in the State to develop national policies, strategies, guidance and programs on radiation protection related matters in different exposure situations to be implemented in the UAE, and to update the same when required;
b) determine the education, training and qualification requirements in the State in the radiation protection field and liaise with the competent, federal and local entities for implementation;
c) adopt recommendations in the areas envisaged in paragraph 3.1 of the ToR which shall be applicable to the entities represented in the Committee (the Represented Authorities), as identified in paragraph 4.2 of the ToR and to other relevant authorities as may be necessary;
d) provide advice to relevant authorities on the means to increase awareness amongst users of radiation sources of good practices in radiation protection and safety culture;
e) approve formation of permanent working groups consisting of experts of the Represented Authorities, approve their terms of reference, and establish, as required, task forces to consider specific topics;
f) consider the recommendations formulated by the working groups or task forces, and as needed provide guidance on the way forward to achieve specific working groups or task forces objectives;
g) monitor the developments in the international system of Radiation Protection and analyse them with the view of their reflection in the UAE regulatory framework;
h) prepare proposals for the UAE Cabinet on radiation protection related issues of a national relevance, following the applicable guidelines established by the Cabinet;
i) review implementation of the ToR and, when necessary, propose amendments to the Chair of the Committee for submission to the Board of Management for approval; and
j) carry out any other functions the Board of Management of FANR assigns to it.
4- Composition of the Committee
4.1- The Committee is formed for a period of three (3) years subject to renewal by the Board of Management of FANR, upon request of the Chair of the Committee.

4.3- The Chair of the Committee shall be the Director General of FANR. The Deputy Chair, who shall be the Director of the Department of Radiation Safety of FANR, shall replace the Chair in his or her absence.
4.4- Each Represented Authority shall have the right to nominate up to three (3) observers. The observers shall have no right to participate in the decisions of the Committee.
4.5- Nomination(s) of representatives of the Represented Authorities and observers to the Committee, and any subsequent changes in such nomination(s), shall be sent by each Represented Authority to the Chair of the Committee in a timely manner before a meeting of the Committee.
4.6- The members of the Committee shall:
a) Be committed to prepare for and attend the Committee meetings;
b) Contribute actively to the work of the Committee and provide a feedback on the documents prepared by the Committee, its working groups or taskforces; and
c) Propose for consideration any topic related to the areas envisaged in paragraph 3 of theToR.
4.7- If a Committee member is absent in two (2) consecutive Committee meetings or three (3) intermittent meetings during the year, the Chair of the Committee shall send a notification to the relevant Represented Authority to take necessary actions in this respect.
5. Quorum and Meetings
Quorum
5.1 A meeting of the Committee shall not be valid unless attended by the majority of its members including the Chair of the Committee (or, in his or her absence, the Deputy Chair).
Meetings
5.2 The Committee shall meet at least once every three (3) months or whenever necessary at the invitation of the Chair of the Committee (or, in his or her absence, the Deputy Chair).
5.3 The Chair of the Committee shall:
a) circulate meeting invitations to the members and observers of the Committee and provide a meeting agenda, summary of the subjects and draft recommendations to be deliberated upon (such invitations and accompanying documents shall be circulated at least one (1) week before the meeting);
b) invite, as may be required, experts to the meetings of the Committee to assist the Committee in its work;
c) receive the nominations of the representative from each Represented Authority in the Committee and monitor the attendance of the Represented Authorities in the meetings of the Committee;
d) submit to the Committee the terms of reference of the working groups envisaged in paragraph 3.2(e) of the ToR;
e) monitor the implementation of the Committee recommendations;
f) submit periodic reports to the Board of Management of FANR on the recommendations of the Committee; and
g) Appoint the Secretary of the Committee from FANR’s employees.
5.4- Upon request of the Board of Management of FANR, the Deputy Chair of the Committee shall report on the activities of the Committee.
5.5- The members of the working groups shall:
a) send recommendations to the Committee within their remit as defined in the working groups terms of reference approved by the Committee;
b) develop a Program for implementing the objectives as specified in their terms of reference within three months of the working group establishment and revise it on a regular basis.
5.6- The Represented Authorities shall bear all costs and expenses incurred by their representatives and observer(s) for participation in the meetings and activities of the Committee.
6- Decisions and Voting
6.1- The Committee shall approve its recommendations and the terms of reference of the working groups by consensus of the members present. In case there is no consensus, recommendations shall be immediately put to the vote and considered adopted in case of a simple majority of affirmative votes of the members present and voting.
6.2- In the event of equal votes (i.e. a tie), the Chair (or, in his or her absence, the Deputy Chair) of the Committee shall have a casting vote and the side of the Chair shall be considered as the side with the greater number of votes in fulfillment of the requirements envisaged in Article 6.1 of these ToR.
6.3- Objecting members shall have the right to register their objections including the reason for objections in the minutes of the meeting.
6.4 The expert(s) invited by the Chair of the Committee and observers nominated by the Represented Authorities to attend meetings of the Committee shall have no right to participate in the decisions of the Committee.
6.5- Approved recommendations shall be forwarded to the Board of Management of FANR for information, endorsement or action, as appropriate.
7- Confidentiality
7.1- Proceedings, correspondence, documentation, information and data in whatsoever format that is related to the work of the Committee shall be treated accordingly, if designated as “For Office Use Only" or “Confidential” by the Committee.
7.2- No person may, without the prior written consent of the Committee, copy or reproduce in any format (in whole or in part) any of the abovementioned information.
8- Responsibilities of the Secretary of the Committee
The Secretary of the Committee shall undertake the following functions:
a) serve as a focal point for the Committee and its members;
b) assist the Chair of the Committee (or, in his or her absence, the Deputy Chair) in the preparation of a meeting agenda, summary of the subjects and draft recommendations to be deliberated upon at the Committee meetings;
c) prepare and circulate on behalf of the Chair of the Committee invitations to the meetings of the Committee;
d) prepare minutes of the Committee meetings, circulate them for comments and signature to the members who attended the meeting, and the Chair or the Deputy Chair of the Committee, as applicable;
e) maintain an updated list of members, experts and observers of the Committee;
f) maintain records of the meetings of the Committee including meeting agenda, summaries of subjects, recommendations, minutes of the meetings; and
g) Perform any other tasks assigned to him or her by the Chair of the Committee (or, in his or her absence, the Deputy Chair).
